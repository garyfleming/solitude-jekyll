Happenings

Film Fight 2024: October

For October, we have six films…

The Platform 2

#FF24 The Platform 2 is similar to the (very good) original but with some twists.

Here the focus is more on doctine, belief, and zeal, than the slightly more random chaos of the original. I wouldn't watch them back to back, but both are worth seeing.

Solid.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 13, 2024

Speak No Evil

#FF24 Speak No Evil is a secluded horror about a family who go somewhere they shouldn't.

McAvoy's shifting accent aside, it keeps the tension high, constantly letting you know that things aren't right, but not stepping back. The ending is a little weak, but doesn't spoil.

Good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 13, 2024

It’s What’s Inside

#FF24 It's What's Inside mixes a simple sci-fi/horror premise with the jealousy of influencer culture.

You can very quickly see the possibilities, and the movie has a lot of fun with the what-ifs. It overcomplicates the ending, with too many expositional twists.

Fun.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 13, 2024

Brothers

#FF24 Brothers is proof a great cast can make a bad film.

Ostensibily a comedy, I don't think I laughed once. The plot is forgettable and the characters are flat - nothing that happened was particularly interesting.

Avoid.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) December 8, 2024

Woman Of The Hour

#FF24 Woman Of The Hour is a bit of a mixed bag.

It's a weird framing (killer on a dating show) of what would have been a better film if told in a more straightforward way. There are moments of levity and moments of well-earned tension, but it's all a bit odd.

Okay.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) December 8, 2024

Strange Darling

#FF24 Strange Darling is a strange but well structured movie.

We start in the middle, and jump backwards and forwards through multiple chapters - that couldn't really be told in any other order. It doesn't quite know when to end, but the journey is well worth it.

Good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) December 8, 2024

The October Winner

Nothing exceptional this month but I think Strange Darling wins it for it’s interesting storytelling.

Film Fight 2024: September

Only four films this month…

Rebel Ridge

#FF24 Rebel Ridge is another classic by Saulnier.

From start to finish, the story and action are both shocking and fantastically executed. Aaron Pierre is a star in the making - absolutely electric in this role.

Go into it blind for a good time.

Great.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 13, 2024

Uglies

#FF24 Uglies is a waste of good streaming bandwidth.

If you told me the script was AI generated with the prompt, "generic young adult dystopian fiction" I would not be shocked. There's not a new idea in here, and plenty that is well-trodden.

Skip.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 13, 2024

The Substance

#FF24 The Substance is a delightful and slightly deranged look at ageing, beauty, and societal pressure.

Moore and Qualley are both fantastic in their respective roles, with Quaid coming in big too. Body horror and satire hits a particularly sweet spot. Again, one to watch cold.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 13, 2024

Wolfs

#FF24 Wolfs is a dry, dark comedy about lone fixers forced to work together.

Pitt and Clooney have good banter together, and the plot has enough layers and threads to keep you going. There's a standout monologue by Austin Abrams too. Runs out of steam towards the end.

Good

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 13, 2024

The September Winner

The Substance could win most months, but Rebel Ridge is too good not to win.

Film Fight 2024: August

Busy month with 11 films in the fight!

Trigger Warning

#FF24 Trigger Warning is the latest Netflix, low-budget action filler.

The acting is flat, the action dull, the plot non-existent. Nothing really holds together in this box-ticking exercise pretending to be a film. Some decent actors absolutely wasted in this.

Avoid.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 18, 2024

The Ministry Of Ungentlemanly Warfare

#FF24 The Ministry Of Ungentlemanly Warfare is a big, glossy, comic-book take on World War 2.

I wasn't shocked when I saw Ritchie had directed it - it has his signature quips and bravado all over it. It's also a little style-over-substance, but as silly action goes, it's decent.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 18, 2024

The Instigators

#FF24 The Instigators is an odd miss.

It's got a solid cast, a good director, and a decent premise. It's just incredibly formulaic. You've seen this done a million times before, and better. Sometimes films aren't as good as the sum of their parts.

Meh.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 18, 2024

The Union

#FF24 The Union is yet another Netflix-backed, formulaic action film.

Wahlberg and Berry make it a bit better than much of the slosh being churned out, and there are some reasonable set pieces. The rest is pretty forgettable.

Meh.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 18, 2024

Jackpot!

#FF24 Jackpot! is a comedy that combines winning the lottery with the purge.

It surprisingly lives up to the bonkers premise, and never takes its foot off the gas. Awkwafina and Cena work well together, with Liu as a nice, late addition. Some fun action too.

Good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 18, 2024

Alien: Romulus

#FF24 Alien: Romulus takes the franchise back to terror in the dark.

A poor choice to deage one actor aside, it treads the line of nostalgia and fan service well, while providing a solid movie. I didn't care for the final act but it doesn't ruin the movie.

Good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 13, 2024

Incoming

#FF24 Incoming is a high-school, gross out comedy that doesn't surprise, but doesn't really have to.

Some fun moments, silly gags, and enough pace to not bore.Clearly the kind of movie you need to be in the right mood for - but perfectly fine if you are.

Fine.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 13, 2024

Bad Boys: Ride Or Die

#FF24 Bad Boys: Ride Or Die continues the style over substance, buddy action franchise.

Very much fits with the others, which have improved somewhat since Bay stepped away. You're going to get big action, some excellent stunts, and nonsense plot.

Switch your brain off and enjoy

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 13, 2024

Blink Twice

#FF24 Blink Twice isn't for the faint-hearted - covering some dark grounds.

While you'll likely figure out what's going on long before the very graphic reveal, the performances are strong enough to carry the film - particularly Ackie, Tatum, and Arjona.

Good, but not easy.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 13, 2024

Trap

#FF24 Trap is silly, Shyamalan nonsense - the cinematic equivalent of buttery popcorn.

The premise makes little sense, but buy into it and go on the ride - it's pretty fun. Hartnett does a great job in the lead role, even if Shyamalan's daughter as the pop star distracts.

Fun.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 13, 2024

Kingdom Of The Planet Of The Apes

#FF24 Kingdom Of The Planet Of The Apes is a mixed bag.

The world that's been built and the tech used to create it is impressive. However, the story doesn't really take us anywhere wildly new. A little too long, too. It has its moments.

Okay.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 13, 2024

The August Winner

Somewhat surprised, but Blink Twice is the best of the bunch.

Film Fight 2024: July

Five films for July!

Kinds of Kindness

#FF24 Kinds of Kindness is an interesting triptych of stories of varying quality.

As with all of Lanthimos' work, the stories and their tone are unusual. The middle story the weakest of the three, but a strong cast makes them all work to some degree. Probably a little long

Good

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 18, 2024

Axel F

#FF24 Axel F is another legacy sequel that works to some degree, but misses in others.

There's a little too much "Remember this?" standing in for plot, but Murphy's charm and comic chops help improve some of the otherwise drudging plot. Could do with being a little shorter

Fine

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 18, 2024

Kill

#FF24 Kill is an exceptionally good action film out of India.

It starts as a fine but weak version of The Raid, but becomes far more gruesome at the midpoint. The plot is barely there, but you won't care as you wince through yet another skull-cracking fight.

Very good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 18, 2024

Maxxxine

#FF24 Maxxxine rounds out the X trilogy with its weakest, but still good, entry.

Mia Goth is exceptional as the lead, leaning into sleazy 80s movie tropes. The problem is there's nothing here to really wow us - the plot is forgettable and it lacks memorable moments.

Okay.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 18, 2024

Deadpool & Wolverine

#FF24 Deadpool & Wolverine is 80% fan service, some okay action, and not much plot.

As long as you go in expecting a bunch of moments that barely hold together, it's quite entertaining - the mid-film extended cameos are excellent, for example.

Entertaining trash.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 18, 2024

The July Winner

I think the winner is Kinds of Kindness - I’d happily watch it again in smaller chunks at home.

Film Fight 2024: June

June has four films for us…

Hit Man

#FF24 Hit Man is a fun watch about a man posing as a hit man for the cops.

Powell is very good as the titular character, with the rest of the cast being solid. It's funny, keeps moving along, and has a handful of light twists. Quite light, but enjoyable.

Good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 17, 2024

The Bikeriders

#FF24 The Bikeriders is a sometimes heavy drama about the formation of a biker gang.

Butler sometimes works as the rebel without a cause, but his nihilism leaves him in a plot rut. Comer and Hardy are more interesting to watch. Gripping and dark.

Excellent.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 17, 2024

Unfrosted

#FF24 Unfrosted is a badly-paced comedy about breakfast.

The plot seems to have been written around bits, rather than being something that makes sense by itself; unfortunately neither entertain. McCarthy is the only performer who comes out looking okay.

Avoid.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 17, 2024

A Quiet Place: Day One

#FF24 A Quiet Place: Day One takes the rural, alien invasion series and moves it to the big city.

The carnage is scaled appropriately, but the human-side is lost. What it gains in spectacle it loses in caring about any of the main characters - well, except the cat.

Fine.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 17, 2024

The June Winner

The only real choice here is The Bikeriders.
