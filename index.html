Film Fight 2022: August

A short month with only 4 films…

Bullet Train

#FF22 Bullet Train is a fine if somewhat forgettable entry into the overstylized action genre.

People have codenames that appear onscreen, we jump around the timeline, the dialogue is snappy: you've seen this before.

Entertaining enough action. Generally fine.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 7, 2022

Prey

#FF22 Prey is an excellent entry in the Predator franchise.

Beautifully shot, well-paced, tense, brutal action. Midthunder is fantastic as the lead, bringing tenacity and vulnerability to the role.

Original and exciting, while bringing back the ethos of the original.

Must see.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 7, 2022

Nope

#FF22 Nope defies neat explanations and simple categorisation.

The cast are strong and it looks great, with the main action being clear and focussed. That recurring sequence in the TV studio is superbly tense. The message is harder to extract.

Enjoyable, but not straightforward

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 20, 2022

Day Shift

#FF22 Day Shift starts with a solid, quiet action scene that sets the world up neatly...

...and quickly devolves. While the action remains decent, the plot is nonsense and most of the characters don't make sense; world-stuffing the film for a franchise.

Forgettably entertaining

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 21, 2022

The August Winner

While Nope is interesting, Prey has to be the winner: a great action film that rejuvenates a flagging franchise.

Film Fight 2022: July

A very light and unimpressive month for July, with just three films.

Thor: Love And Thunder

#FF22 Thor: Love And Thunder is about as paint by numbers as Marvel gets.

It takes no chances, does nothing fresh. The plot and character development are woefully undercooked too: Bale is solid as Gorr, but it doesn't give him much time to build up. Some fun moments.

Meh.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 24, 2022

Where The Crawdads Sing

#FF22 Where The Crawdads Sing is comically bad.

The dialogue and performances are close to parody territory with only Strathairn any good. The plot is as silly as it is predictable. I'm not bothered about not seeing what actually happened, because I'd long stopped caring.

Avoid

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 24, 2022

The Gray Man

#FF22 The Gray Man doesn't do much with its strong cast.

The plot is straight from the action-film-script generator, with action that matches, and little in the way of originality or flair anywhere. Setup, location, set-piece, over and over, with little connection.

Skip.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 7, 2022

The July Winner

A pretty grim month, so Thor: Love And Thunder inexplicably wins by being the only film I might possibly watch a second time many years from now.

Film Fight 2022: June

A fairly short Film Fight for June, with just 5 films.

Men

#FF22 Men is not a subtle film in the slightest.

It is, however, an effective small horror, with exceptionally strong performances by the two leads, and several visceral and creepy moments that will live in my memory.

A little slow towards the end, but decent.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) June 12, 2022

Spiderhead

#FF22 Spiderhead takes a broad look at abuses in pharmaceuticals, focussing on issues of control like a lightweight Milgram experiment.

The cast are fine, seemingly having fun, and the runtime passes quickly, but the film doesn't have much to say in the end.

Okay.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 3, 2022

Pleasure

#FF22 Pleasure focusses on issues of consent and power in the adult entertainment industry.

Bella's journey is not an easy watch, each step taking her further into that world. Thyberg's direction/writing is even-handed: neither celebrating or condeming it.

Good, not easy.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 3, 2022

Top Gun: Maverick

#FF22 Top Gun: Maverick is very much an 80s film in modern clothing.

The plot is silly and the machismo is over the top, with too many nods to the original. But it's still a lot of fun. The action sequences are predictable, but spectacular, and it all comes together.

Fun.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 3, 2022

Hustle

#FF22 Hustle follows a basketball scout, looking for his next break.

This is a serious Sandler film, without being dour. It pulls together several underdog and sport movie plots into something new enough. There are laughs, difficulties, and triumphs that keep it moving.

Solid.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 3, 2022

The June Winner

A tricky one because there’s plenty of good but nothing excellent here. I think the movie I’d see again soonest is Men, so that’s the winner.

Film Fight 2022: May

Seven films for May.

All The Old Knives

#FF22 All The Old Knives is a very solid, mid-budget, spy thriller.

It, rightly, focusses on espionage and intrigue, rather than action. The cast (Chris Pine, Thandiwe Newton) do a great job of pulling you into the drama, as the narrative threads shift around.

Worth seeing.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) May 7, 2022

The Contractor

#FF22 The Contractor fails to do much interesting with its premise.

The slow and uneven pace don't help with the vague stakes, and predictable plot turns. The action lacks energy; with most sequences feeling very staged. The cast can't do much.

It's not bad, it's very average.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) May 8, 2022

Doctor Strange In The Multiverse Of Madness

#FF22 Doctor Strange In The Multiverse Of Madness is fine, if you enjoy Marvel films.

Raimi does a great job of injecting an 80s horror vibe, with a surprising amount of violence for the rating. It's a relative tame take on the multiverse, and not one well-explored

Fine.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) May 15, 2022

Operation Mincemeat

#FF22 Operation Mincemeat is a historical drama focussing on espionage in WW2.

While it tells the story well enough, it doesn't go into any depth. All of the sharp edges have been sanded down, and a by-numbers romantic subplot bolted on. The cast are fine.

Decent if lightweight

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) May 15, 2022

Everything Everywhere All At Once

#FF22 Everything Everywhere All At Once is the kind of weird, fun sci-fi we need more of.

The cast helps the emotional weight of the story shine through, where it might have been lost to the wacky concepts. The directors are showing off with great nods to world cinema

Excellent

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) May 15, 2022

Jackass 4.5

#FF22 Jackass 4.5 does largely what you expect. As with the other .5 increments, it's a mix of making-of documentary and unused footage from the main film.

It's fun to see the chaotic thinking behind the scenes, but isn't going to be for everyone.

Fine.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) June 12, 2022

Emergency

#FF22 Emergency plays with its tone well.

It has the setup of a college comedy, with an elaborate drinking quest but quickly it becomes something far more serious, flipping between moods.

Solid performances throughout but the fear/raw emotion in the last act is stunning.

Watch

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) June 12, 2022

The May Winner

The May winner is the unexpected brilliance of Everything Everywhere All At Once.

Film Fight 2022: April

Only five films in this fight, but they’re mostly good.

Titane

#FF22 Titane is a strange film that deserves it's Cronenbergian nods.

A mish-mash of violence and movement makes up a movie concerned with bodies and what they should and should not do; about love, and need. Beyond flat description.

Strange.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) April 2, 2022

Apollo 10 1/2: A Space Age Childhood

#FF22 Apollo 10 1/2: A Space Age Childhood brings together a few Linklater tropes pleasantly.

It's an easy going look at a moment in time, with rotoscoped animation making it seem further away. The narration didn't work for me, but the plot progresses nicely enough.

Decent.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) April 17, 2022

The Northman

#FF22 The Northman is not a subtle film.

At times mythic and supernatural, at others full of brutality grounded by those excellent one-shots. It's silly, weird, and gripping. Skarsgård is excellent as the ball of rage at the heart of the film.

Very good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) April 17, 2022

The Bubble

#FF22 The Bubble tries to have fun with its premise, but isn't fully cooked.

There are some decent, knowing jokes, but far too few for the runtime. There's not much of a plot, more of a bunch of sequences they wanted in place.

Underwritten, too long, and not much fun. Skip.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) April 30, 2022

Mass

#FF22 Mass is an incredible film about loss and anger, and forgiveness.

The style subtly shifts as the mood changes but four powerhouse performances are at the centre of the film, with a single location letting the film focus on the actors.

Film of the year contender.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) May 1, 2022

The April Winner

While The Northman was winning until late on the last day of the month, Mass is the clear winner; a difficult film that will stay with me for years to come.
