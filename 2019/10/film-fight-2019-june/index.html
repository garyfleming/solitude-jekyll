Film Fight 2019: June

A very quiet month for June, with just three films.

I Am Mother

#FF19 I Am Mother, as lo-fi, nearly single-location, sci-fi goes, is pretty decent. Following a girl raised underground by a robot after an unseen apocalypse, it builds tension well.

Some of the plot beats are a little predictable, but the actresses sell everything. Worth seeing

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) June 18, 2019

Booksmart

#FF19 Booksmart is funny and charming, and awkward, and fun. The references to Superbad are understandable but don't do it justice: it's doing something a little different, but with some of the same DNA.

A great directorial debut, a memorable comedy, and a decent film. See it.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) June 18, 2019

Brightburn

#FF19 Brightburn has a solid premise (what if, as a boy, Clark Kent, was a sociopath?), but squanders it to some extent. The gruesome horror bits work well, but the film fails to build a meaningful story around them.

Solid enough, but very set-piece driven.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) June 21, 2019

The June Winner

The winner for June is Booksmart, a great comedy with some nice character moments.
