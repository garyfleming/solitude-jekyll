Happenings

Film Fight 2019: September

A very small month for September, with just two films.

Between Two Ferns

#FF19 Between Two Ferns: The Movie is pretty much what you’d expect if you watched the series. Awkward interviews, some laughs, a super-thin plot.

Light entertainment but not much more.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) September 30, 2019

In The Shadow Of The Moon

#FF19 In The Shadow Of The Moon is a decent enough detective thriller with a sci-fi bent.

The twists are a little predictable and info dumped, but the film is reasonably well made.

Worth seeing.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) September 30, 2019

The September Winner

The winner for September is In The Shadow Of The Moon. Even though I’d like to have seen a slightly higher-budget take, this was a solid piece of sci-fi.

Film Fight 2019: August

A reasonable month with four films for August.

Once Upon A Time In Hollywood

#FF19 Once Upon A Time In Hollywood is difficult. On one hand, it's self-indulgent, needlessly lengthy, doesn't particularly go anywhere, and has a cop-out ending.

On the other, it's beautifully shot, has a fun relationship at its core, and some fantastic scenes.

Mixed feelings

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 30, 2019

American Factory

#FF19 American Factory is a documentary about the culture clash between Chinese and American works at a newly reopened factory in Ohio.

The perspective is relatively neutral. It's somewhat slow-paced. There's no over-produced drama.

It's a pleasant enough watch for the length.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 30, 2019

Point Blank

#FF19 Point Blank is an action film with a generic plot and dialogue that'll never surprise you.

Some of the car sequences are decent, but over-edited. Some of the fights work well. Some of the framing is actually pretty good.

It's entirely forgettable but watchable for 90 mins

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 30, 2019

Good Boys

#FF19 Good Boys is an entirely forgettable comedy about kids getting ready for big moments in their lives.

It never really gets any big laughs, but there are enough small jokes scattered throughout that it's not a chore.

Fine, but probably watch-once material.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 31, 2019

The August Winner

Again, nothing that really shines in August. I’m surprised to say that Once Upon A Time In Hollywood has won.

Film Fight 2019: July

Another quiet month with just three films…

Spider-Man: Far From Home

#FF19 Spider-Man: Far From Home is the MCU film you deserve right now. Light-hearted action, funny quips, decent characters.

There's nothing new here, but it's done well. The twists are obvious (I guessed most from the trailers). The film is fun. Watch it.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 8, 2019

The Dead Don’t Die

#FF19 The Dead Don’t Die is a weird wink and nod to Romero’s movies, without saying anything fresh.

A little self-knowing. Funny, with some great characters (fantastically cast). It doesn’t really go anywhere but it really doesn’t have to.

If you love Jarmusch, you’ll like this

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 13, 2019

The Current War

#FF19 The Current War is a decent enough telling of the fight between Westinghouse and Edison.

Performances are fine, but it could build out the characters a bit more. The editing needs to give scenes more room to breath, and the tealy/orange colour grading could use work.

Ok

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 28, 2019

The July Winner

Not a particularly strong month, so I think the winner is the fun action of Spider-Man: Far From Home.

Film Fight 2019: June

A very quiet month for June, with just three films.

I Am Mother

#FF19 I Am Mother, as lo-fi, nearly single-location, sci-fi goes, is pretty decent. Following a girl raised underground by a robot after an unseen apocalypse, it builds tension well.

Some of the plot beats are a little predictable, but the actresses sell everything. Worth seeing

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) June 18, 2019

Booksmart

#FF19 Booksmart is funny and charming, and awkward, and fun. The references to Superbad are understandable but don't do it justice: it's doing something a little different, but with some of the same DNA.

A great directorial debut, a memorable comedy, and a decent film. See it.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) June 18, 2019

Brightburn

#FF19 Brightburn has a solid premise (what if, as a boy, Clark Kent, was a sociopath?), but squanders it to some extent. The gruesome horror bits work well, but the film fails to build a meaningful story around them.

Solid enough, but very set-piece driven.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) June 21, 2019

The June Winner

The winner for June is Booksmart, a great comedy with some nice character moments.

Film Fight 2019: May

A huge month for Film Fight. We’re into double-digits with 10 films for May.

The Wandering Earth

#FF19 The Wandering Earth is attempting to be a blockbuster through scale, but lacks the stakes.

The film is spectacle first, plot second, characters… eventually.

It’s a light enough watch but not terribly entertaining. Looks cheap. Feels underwritten.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) May 7, 2019

Prospect

#FF19 Prospect is indie sci-fi done very well.

The premise and stakes are clear, but rarely do we get an exposition dump.

It looks gorgeous. The old west prospecting future is somewhere I’d gladly revisit, with lots of weird wrinkles.

An excellent, tight movie. See it.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) May 7, 2019

Long Shot

#FF19 Long Shot is a so-so comedy. It's got some great moments, but not particularly well-linked. Charlize Theron is excellent in it, and Seth Rogen is... well, Seth Rogen.

Not a terrible film, but not particularly memorable.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) May 13, 2019

Wine Country

#FF19 Wine Country is a little flatter than the cast suggests. Trying to give every cast member an arc is admirable, but means none of them really get the time they need to stretch.

Funny moments, but rarely laugh out loud. Watchable.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) May 13, 2019

John Wick: Chapter 3 - Parabellum

John Wick: Chapter 3 - Parabellum has fewer big action sequences, and a lot more up-close combat. From the early library fight to the ending, there's little room to breath. The action is excellent.

If you like the earlier films, this is more of the same in the best possible way.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) May 18, 2019

Maria

#FF19 Maria is a poor martial arts film. The plot is paper thin, the acting seems pretty bad, and the characters and their motivations seem to be whatever will get the plot moving.

Some of the action sequences are cleanly executed and edited. Others have too many cuts. Skip.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) May 23, 2019

Knock Down The House

#FF19 Knock Down the House is an inspirational, but somewhat dry, documentary on the movement to improve candidates to the senate.

With common knowledge, it's robbed of its dramatic beats, but still shows us the ups and downs of several important campaigns. Decent.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) May 29, 2019

The Perfection

#FF19 The Perfection is... not good in the most bizarre car crash fashion. Beautifully shot, but a mess of themes and messages. Some things that happen are problematic, some are bizarre, whilst others are plain funny.

It's a 90 minute mess. Decide for yourself.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) May 29, 2019

The Furie

#FF19 The Furie is slow to get started, and has a few dreary sections in the middle, with a somewhat generic plot.

That said, the martial arts work, and the accompanying camera moves are excellent. Kinetic, motived, interesting. Better than most fighting films.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) May 31, 2019

Rim of the Wolrd

#FF19 Rim of the World is a bit of a mess. At times it's a YA drama about an alien invasion, at others it's making crude yet cliched jokes; with all the character development of a kids film.

I've no idea who this is for. That said, it's somewhat entertaining in places. Meh.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) June 1, 2019

The May Winner

The winner for May is indie sci-fi meets Western, Prospect. A tight film, well made.
