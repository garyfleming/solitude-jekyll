Happenings

Film Fight 2023: March

Just six films in the fight this month…

Creed 3

#FF23 Creed 3 is typical of the franchise: the ups and downs of fighting someone stronger, rising to the challenge, and succeeding in some way.

The cast are all solid, the plot is predictable, and everything is watchable. The visual flair in the last act is cringeworthy.

Fine.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) March 12, 2023

65

#FF23 65 treats it's somewhat ridiculous premise (space man crash lands on a dinosaur filled Earth) with an even tone: serious without being dour, not as a gag.

The runtime feels right, and Driver holds it together well enough. You'll see every twist coming but won't mind.

Okay

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) March 12, 2023

The Boston Strangler

#FF23 The Boston Strangler is a reasonable enough take on the reporting around the eponymous killer.

Both Knightley and Coon are solid as the journalists tracking down leads. It looks fine, but very generic: tealy/orange-tinged period drama.

Nothing special, but decent enough.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) March 19, 2023

John Wick: Chapter 4

#FF23 John Wick: Chapter 4 is exactly what you want in an action film.

It's a little too long, but is otherwise excellent: some of the best action and stunts in cinema, shot clearly and inventively, with a bunch of performers showing off what they can do.

Really good fun.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) April 2, 2023

Infinity Pool

#FF23 Infinity Pool is creeping body horror with something to say about predatorial tourism.

Goth and Skarsgård are both fully committed to their weird roles, delivering an unease that slowly ramps up. The pacing is a bit off and it doesn't get as weird as it might.

Pretty good

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) April 2, 2023

Women Talking

#FF23 Women Talking is disarmingly calm, for the most part, despite the horrific impetus.

A quiet conversation for 90 or so minutes, with some excellent performances from the entire cast. Despite the one major location, it remains grounded and never feels stagey.

Very good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) April 2, 2023

The March Winner

Some decent films in March, but the best film here is clearly Women Talking.

Film Fight 2023: February

Seven films in the February Film Fight.

The Whale

#FF23 The Whale is a real mix.

Fraser is excellent in a layered performance, that is empathetic. The rest of the characters are underwritten to the point of being one note, or have bizarrely, contrived arcs. Works well in moments, but falls apart as a whole.

Meh.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) February 5, 2023

Bank of Dave

#FF23 Bank Of Dave is about a man trying to create a bank to help working class people.

While the fish-out-of-water jokes are tired and the romantic subplot forced, the whole thing works as a feel-good, light, underdog story. It won't win any awards, but it's easy and solid.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) February 13, 2023

All Quiet On The Western Front

#FF23 All Quiet On The Western Front is a protracted look at the horrors of war, and the loss of innocence.

It treads no new ground, but it covers the ground it does spectacularly and devastatingly. It makes trench warfare personal and tense, with some horrifying moments.

Great

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) February 13, 2023

Knock At The Cabin

#FF23 Knock At The Cabin sees Shyamalan back on form with a small, contained horror thriller that lingers in psychological doubts.

Batista is excellent as a gentle but terrifying force, delivering an apocalyptic premise. The end is heavy-handed, but the rest works well.

Good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) February 13, 2023

Ant Man & The Wasp: Quantumania

#FF23 Ant Man & The Wasp: Quantumania is the blandest Marvel film in a while.

Jonathan Majors is great in his role, but the film itself is all world-building and no interesting plot or ideas. It lacks the humour and originality of the previous films.

Boring and disappointing.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) February 26, 2023

Sharper

#FF23 Sharper does so well for a lot of its runtime, and then squanders it in the ending.

You'll see most of the cons coming, but they're executed well. The cast are excellent and make it work, but the last act requires them to become very stupid. Doesn't work.

Okay.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) February 26, 2023

Cocaine bear

#FF23 Cocaine Bear is everything I expected it to be.

The title does most of the work: a comedic horror about a black bear finding quite a lot of coke and going on a rampage. It's over-the-top, fun, a bit silly, and a bit gory. Easy watching.

Enjoyable.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) February 26, 2023

The February Winner

While Cocaine Bear nearly got it for it’s simple entertainment value, I think the winner has to be All Quiet On The Western Front, a difficult but powerful watch.

Film Fight 2023: January

A good start to the year with seven films in the fight.

The Pale Blue Eye

#FF23 The Pale Blue Eye is a decent enough thriller, following a bizarre crime and threads that don't quite match up.

The last act doesn't work, and is on the long side, but the rest is solid enough. The leads put in good performances selling the faux-supernatural elements

Okay

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) January 22, 2023

Empire of Light

#FF23 Empire Of Light is a love letter to the cinema experience, the racism of the 80s, mental health issues...

Its biggest problem is that it has way too many plot threads. Colman and Ward are both very good, but nothing gets a chance to breath. No subtlety either.

Fine.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) January 22, 2023

Tár

#FF23 Tár is an excellent film styled as a biopic of a masterful, but deeply flawed, conductor.

Blanchett puts in an incredible performance, even by her own standards, with direction to match. The oner at Julliard is jaw dropping. Worth every minute of the lengthy runtime

Great

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) January 22, 2023

Babylon

#FF23 Babylon is a mess of a movie.

There are fun, big, extended sequences, and solid moments throughout, but nothing holds together. The cast are having fun, and it's an interesting period, but there's no real story here.

There might be a film in here if you cut an hour.

Skip

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) January 22, 2023

M3gan

#FF23 M3GAN is probably exactly what you expect if you've seen a trailer.

A fun, light horror about an increasingly dangerous doll, like Child's Play. It's entertaining, well made, and more than a bit silly. Williams does well in finding the right tone.

Fun.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) January 29, 2023

The Fabelmans

#FF23 The Fabelmans is a light biopic of Spielberg's barely-disguised upbringing.

The cast are excellent, particularly Monaghan as the artistic, flighty mother. Throughout we see the awe Sammy does in the world of film, but without much weight.

Okay, but toothless.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) January 29, 2023

Aftersun

#FF23 Aftersun follows a father and daughter on a 90s holiday.

Its true marvel is there's no dark narrative drama. It just shows an honest slice of a relationship, with great, naturalistic performances. It captures the feel of a home movie, intercutting DV footage.

Very good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) February 5, 2023

The January Winner

Despite some very good films, this is an easy win for Tár: incredible performances, good drama, gorgeous film.

Film Fight 2022: Finale

Another year of film watching done. Back to the cinema for a lot of this, but also a bunch that were first/early release at home. Seems like a lot of low to mid budget films are skipping cinemas entirely these days.

As per the caveat every year, Film Fight is done in a (non particularly serious) knock-out style: it can pick my favourite film of the year, but nothing else. (Also the very idea of a single favourite film is a bit silly.)

First up is the honorable mentions: films that weren’t good enough to win their own month, but still worth seeing.

  • Licorice Pizza
  • Swan Song
  • The Eyes Of Tammy Faye
  • Petite Maman
  • Belfast

  • After Yang

  • Emergency

  • Rogue Agent
  • Three Thousand Years of Longing
  • I Came By

  • Emily The Criminal
  • Clerks III
  • The Menu
  • The Wonder
  • Bones And All
  • The Stranger
  • She Said
  • Glass Onion
  • Corsage

And now the monthly winners:

  • January: The Lost Daughter
  • February: CODA
  • March: Kimi
  • April: Mass
  • May: Everything Everywhere All At Once
  • June: Men
  • July: Thor: Love And Thunder (no, I wasn’t happy about it either)
  • August: Prey
  • September: The Forgiven
  • October: Barbarian
  • November: Pearl
  • December: Decision To Leave

We have a decent enough list of winners with only July really letting the side down.

CODA and Mass were excellent character and performance driven films, with important but very different messages.

There’s more horror on the list than in previous years, with Barbarian being the best of three, but Pearl being pretty close, especially for that monologue at the end. Men was fine, but I don’t see myself going back to it much.

The Forgiven and Decision To Leave have you sitting in a feeling considering what you might do in this situation, which makes for great, lingering cinema.

I’m quite torn. I saw Decision To Leave a few days ago so it’s still quite fresh, but I also remember the fairly emotional experiences of watching CODA and Mass.

I think I’m going with Mass as my film of 2022. It’s a simple setup, mostly four people in one room talking about a tragedy, but you feel every bit of it.

Film Fight 2022: December

For December, we have 9 films in the fight!

Violent Night

#FF22 Violent Night tries to make a festive John Wick, and doesn't quite manage.

A fun premise but, despite some enjoyable moments, the action doesn't really work: way too many cuts, and under-lit scenes for it to be slick. Harbour is solid enough.

Okay but largely forgettable

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) December 4, 2022

The Stranger

#FF22 The Stranger is a slow, unnerving film that slowly lets you in on the horrendous case hidden behind the immediate narrative.

Both Harris and Edgerton are good, as the creepy con and undercover cop. A very slow burn, as we start to understand the scope.

Good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) December 4, 2022

She Said

#FF22 She Said is about the investigative journalists who break the Weinstein story.

It's very straightforward, and fast-paced; constantly moving to the next lead. It focuses, rightly, on the women impacted, including the journalists. Good central performances.

Worth seeing.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) December 11, 2022

The Infernal Machine

#FF22 The Infernal Machine is pretty dull and slow.

Pierce is fine as the lead, but he's not given a lot to go on. The twists are either very signposted or absolutely baffling, including the cut-rate Stephen King ending.

Skip.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) December 30, 2022

Glass Onion

#FF22 Glass Onion: A Knives Out Mystery is fun, if lightweight, entertainment.

The entire cast is great, especially Monae and Craig; mostly playing unlikeable, vain people. The structure works well, with some neat twists hidden in plain sight.

Enjoyable.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) December 30, 2022

Corsage

#FF22 Corsage is a slow but very watchable look at rejecting what's expected of you, while still needing others.

Vicki Krieps is excellent as the lead (an Austrian Empress). The whole thing looks great, with the interesting choice of non-period elements coming through.

Good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) January 7, 2023

It’s A Wonderful Binge

#FF22 It's A Wonderful Binge is genuinely terrible.

Where the first one was stupid but fun, this is just an incoherent mess; none of the main threads really work or come together. Few if any funny moments. Kaitlin Olson is better than this.

Skip.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) January 7, 2023

White Noise

#FF22 White Noise is turgidly slow, and lacks .

It tries an affect somewhere between the Coens and Wes Anderson, failing entirely. The dialogue is read in the same flat way by every character. The few good moments don't save it.

Surprisingly bad, given the cast and director.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) January 7, 2023

Decision to Leave

#FF22 Decision To Leave is another masterpiece by Park Chan-wook.

The story and imagery constantly brings us back to the themes (failing love, new love, betrayal etc), showing us everything from fresh perspectives.

Melancholy, but stunningly executed. Will stay with you.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) January 7, 2023

The December Winner

The December winner is the excellent Decision To Leave, largely for the reason stated: you’re not going to forget this soon.
