Happenings

Film Fight 2020: October

My concentration was elsewhere so only three films in October.

Enola Holmes

#FF20 Enola Holmes didn't work for me. While the cast are charming, the plot they're running through feels forced and overlong; a series of set pieces and encounters, flimsily connected. None of the characters earned their emotional beats.

Honestly, a bit dull.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 11, 2020

The Other Lamb

#FF20 The Other Lamb follows a cult member starting to become disillusioned with her leader.

This film is gorgeous: from the saturated colours against the natural, to some of the vistas. Stunning cinematogrpahy.

The plot is lacking. The changes and ending feel cliched. Decent.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 26, 2020

The Trial of The Chicago 7

#FF20 The Trial Of The Chicago 7 is a look at government power, protest, and counter-culture, through the lens of a courtroom drama.

Strong performances from an excellent cast, tight dialogue, and excellent structuring have the film going at a great pace.

Very good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 31, 2020

The October Winner

The winner for October is The Trial Of The Chicago 7, a timely reminder of systemic injustice.

Film Fight 2020: September

A reasonable month, with 6 new films…

Get Duked

#FF20 Get Duked is a decent dark comedy, that clearly takes inspiration from Hot Fuzz, both tonally and visually.

It's not quite as slick as Wright's work, but has some very funny moments, neat visuals, and a fun plot.

A solid feature debut. Good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) September 6, 2020

I’m Thinking of Ending Things

#FF20 I'm Thinking of Ending Things is a difficult movie.

The conversation at the start is excellent, and builds tension, warps into unease, and a creeping horror. The last act drops the ball, focussing on indulgence, not delivery.

Performances are all excellent.

Very mixed.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) September 6, 2020

Spree

#FF20 Spree is a fairly broad and somewhat satirical look at what streaming and influencer culture does to people, as we follow a guy who decides on a dangerous path forward.

Reasonably well shot, decent pacing, and a bit silly. It's all fairly predictable. Okay.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) September 27, 2020

The Devil All The Time

#FF20 The Devil All The Time is less than the sum of its parts.

A really good cast are mired in various plots we've seen before that tie together in a way that seems forced. It suffers from horrible narration: have the actors show me, don't tell me.

Pretty forgettable. Skip.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) September 27, 2020

Freaks: You’re One Of Us

#FF20 Freaks: You're One Of Us is a German superhero story about people whose powers have been suppressed.

It's fine, if entirely predictable and chock full of stereotypes. There are a few neat moments, but mostly this is just mild entertainment.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 3, 2020

Bill & Ted Face The Music

#FF20 Bill & Ted Face The Music is everything no-one needed. It brings back old characters with nods and winks, adds new ones that are uninteresting, and mostly tries to get by on nostalgia.

Twenty minutes in you'll know exactly what's going to happen.

Tired and dull.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 3, 2020

The September Winner

Turns out there wasn’t much worthwhile this month, so the winner is Get Duked.

Film Fight 2020: August

A little quieter than recent months, but still 7 new films.

7500

#FF20 7500 says Joseph Gordon-Levitt as a co-pilot fighting off a hijacking attempt.

A solid one location film, with great sound design, tense moments, and some neat plotting. The last 20-30 minutes drag a little, but it holds up overall.

Worth seeing.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 9, 2020

The Vast of Night

#FF20 The Vast of Night never quite reaches where it should.

The performances are fine with some very neat shots (and one that is a bit too clever), but the script mistakes running frantically between places with action. Much of the story is flat and expositional.

Meh.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 9, 2020

Project Power

#FF20 Project Power is a bit of a mess.

Despite a solid cast, it's a bit of a mess. The script feels underwritten, bumbling from set piece to set piece in a clunky way.

The action itself is bad: a mess of edits that makes it difficult to enjoy any of it.

Really not good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 16, 2020

Days of the Bagnold Summer

#FF20 Days of the Bagnold Summer is a charming story about a single mum and her metalhead son.

It perfectly plays teenage grump and awkwardness against a librarian in cardigan. The neat cinematography provides lots of beautiful shots and framing.

Funny, sweet, and worth seeing.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 22, 2020

An American Pickle

#FF20 An American Pickle is a fish out of water buddy comedy, about a man preserved in brine for 100 years.

It has moments of humour, but is ultimately a bit one note. Neither of Seth Rogan's characters are particularly 2-dimensional.

Meh.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 22, 2020

Never Rarely Sometimes Always

#FF20 Never Rarely Sometimes Always is an important look at how difficult it can be to get an abortion in the US.

Shot in a grounded style, the quietness amplifies the reality being shown. The performances are equally subtle.

Sometimes difficult viewing, but worth seeing.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 31, 2020

Tenet

#FF29 Tenet is a Nolan film, through and through.

Spectacular action and a complicated narrative, with a great performance by Washington. Also suffers from being excessively expositional, and having terrible sound design, as with most of his films.

Worth seeing at least once.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 31, 2020

The August Winner

The winner for August is Never Rarely Sometimes Always, a difficult but important watch, that’s well put together.

Film Fight 2020: July

A fairly respectable month, with 10 films in July.

Family Romance, LLC

#FF20 Family Romance, LLC is a fascinating look at Japanese businesses who rent family members, like a father for your wedding.

Presented in a documentary style, the drama that unfolds is subtle and human. Herzog makes his protagonist relatable, despite his business.

Very good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 5, 2020

Wasp Network

#FF20 Wasp Network has a good first hour, setting up several characters in this story about how the Cuban government would infiltrate organisations in Florida.

The second hour? Feels like they really need a lot longer.

Solid performances, but doesn't really get time to develop.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 11, 2020

Hamilton

#FF20 Hamilton lives up to its reputation as an exceptionally polished musical, mixing styles to create something fresh.

Even at 3 hours, it rushes a lot, with a lot of tell-don't-show storytelling. Lots of characters, particularly the women, are pretty flat.

Good but flawed.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 11, 2020

The Old Guard

#FF20 The Old Guard lives up to its billing: a Summer blockbuster for lockdown. Not as spectacular as it might be, but never looks cheap.

The premise is decent, but some of the worldbuilding a bit heavyhanded. Some decent action. Some not so decent.

Fun enough for two hours.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 12, 2020

Palm Springs

#FF20 Palm Springs sits neatly in one of my favourite tropes: time-loops. This is Groundhog Day at a wedding, if Andie MacDowell's character was also in on the loop.

Funny, sweet, dumb, inventive, and with a bunch of neat twists.

Samberg and Milioti make this great.

Very good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 12, 2020

The Assistant

#FF20 The Assistant is a look at how people become complicit in sexual harassment, through utter mundanity and bullying.

The first 30 minutes work without words: painting a picture of the drudge of office life, with clues sprinkled around.

Fantastic performances. Must see.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 19, 2020

Nobody Knows I’m Here

#FF20 Nobody Knows I'm Here is about a reclusive former child singer.

Each new bit of information about his circumstances complicates the story just enough to make it interesting again.

Good lead performance. Ends at the right time. Worth seeing.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 19, 2020

VFW

#FF20 VFW is about veterans protecting a girl from drug-fuelled, almost zombie-like punks.

It's intentionally trashy, b-movie fodder. Grim, gory, and over-the-top in every respect.

The dialogue is awful and the characters unpleasant. Decent practical effects.

Mindless.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 26, 2020

Only

#FF20 Only is about a global pandemic that infects women, and a couple fighting for survival.

It's a not particularly subtle take on patriarchy and misogyny, going back and forward over the first year after the outbreak.

Okay in most regards.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 26, 2020

The Night Clerk

#FF20 The Night Clerk is a dull, lifestyle movie that has somehow got a solid cast.

That said, the cast are given very little to do, other than crawl through the boring, predictable plot at a snail's pace.

Entirely forgettable. Avoid.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 1, 2020

The July Winner

Tough to pick between the three films that stood out, but I think it has to be The Assistant. Fantastic but understated performances that show us how we become complicit in things with which we disagree.

Film Fight 2020: June

A slightly quieter month than last, but still 8 new films!

The Orphanage

#FF20 The Orphanage follows an Afghan boy forced into a Russian-run Orphanage in the 1980s.

It switches between slice of life moments, surviving with bullies, to fantastical musical and action numbers, without breaking a sweat.

At times delightful, and others brutal.

Very good

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) June 4, 2020

The Wrong Missy

#FF20 The Wrong Missy is moderately better than the sum of its parts. Where most recent Happy Madison comedies have been terrible, this is... fine

A little wrong-headed in places, but funny in others. If it's late, you've had a drink, and want something easy, this might work. Ok

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) June 16, 2020

Da 5 Bloods

#FF20 Da 5 Bloods is a solid film about friendship, ageing, trauma, and what motivates us.

The central cast are great, funny and well-fleshed out. The peripheral characters less so.

Longer than it needs to be, but not aggrevatingly so. A solid film.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) June 16, 2020

The Banker

#FF20 The Banker is a solid, historical drama about two of the earliest Black Americans to own a bank.

The core cast, Mackie in particular, are good, the pacing works well, and you'll be charmed by how they figure out how to beat a system that is against them.

Very watchable.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) June 28, 2020

Krabi, 2562

#FF20 Krabi, 2562 is a distant look at tourism and Thailand, against the backdrop of advertising, mysticism, and an unusual woman.

It sounds better than it is. Lingering shots of the environment are beautiful but boring. Nothing really goes anywhere, it's more about mood.

Skip.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) June 28, 2020

The Last Days of American Crime

#FF20 The Last Days of American Crime: I can accept the silly premise.

I can’t accept the awful dialogue, empty action, mountains of cliched plotting, awful characters, exposition, languid pacing etc.

If you’re going to overproduce a heist movie, do it in 90 minutes. Not 150.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) June 28, 2020

Eurovision Song Contest: The Story of Fire Saga

#FF20 Eurovision Song Contest: The Story of Fire Saga is an almost entirely forgettable comedy.

The idea is fine, but there are too few laughs and too many things we've seen before for it to be lasting.

Dan Stevens and Graham Norton have the best moments.

Meh.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) June 28, 2020

The Ghost Who Walks

#FF20 The Ghost Who Walks follows a recently released convict trying to reconnect with his family, and avoid a hit.

It looks good, particularly the lighting and framing in action sequences, but it doesn't deliver. The wider cast are not great, and the writing is cliched.

Meh.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 5, 2020

The June Winner

Lots of decidedly mediocre films, a couple of decent ones, but the winner is worth seeing: The Orphanage takes it.
