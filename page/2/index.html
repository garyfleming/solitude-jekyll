Film Fight 2022: October

A busy month with 10 new films…

Don’t Worry Darling

#FF22 Don't Worry Darling is an entertaining mess of a film.

The production design and general look of the film is top notch, with real Bioshock vibes. Florence Pugh, too, is excellent. The twist is predictable, but aspects of it are eye-rollingly bad.

Good enough, but light.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 2, 2022

Hatching

#FF22 Hatching is a fun, if at sometimes, silly horror.

A young, Finnish girl rescues a motherless egg, and in the process creates something creepy. The performances and pacing are solid, but there's very little subtext here; everything is pretty clear.

Good enough.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 16, 2022

The Woman King

#FF22 The Woman King is an excellent, historical action film, with a great cast.

One light romantic subplot aside, the various threads all work well. The film fleshes out its characters fully, beyond just being incredibly tough women. Looks fantastic too.

Great action film.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 16, 2022

Luckiest Girl Alive

#FF22 Luckiest Girl Alive is a bit of mess.

It jumps into heavy-handed narration, that tells rather than shows, and has some very out of place moments. There are a few scenes with appropriate weight, but they're the exception. Tonally all over the place.

Fine.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 16, 2022

No Exit

#FF22 No Exit knows what it is: a lightweight, low-runtime, strangers in a cabin horror-thriller. On that level, it works.

The film won't surprise you much, the strokes being used here are broad, but it does its thing well. There are a handful of well-done gruesome moments.

Ok

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 16, 2022

Amsterdam

#FF22 Amsterdam is a mess on all levels.

An incredible cast who are tonally in different films; a meandering plot with leaden pacing; showy direction that distracts; horrible structure with intermittent narration; and wildly unnatural dialogue.

Some funny lines, but pretty bad.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 16, 2022

The Banshees of Inisherin

#FF22 The Banshees Of Inisherin is a bleak, black comedy in the vein of McDonagh's other works.

The performances here work well, espcially Farrell's nice but dim farmer and Gleeson's dour and uptight musician. Some difficult moments punctuated with real laughs.

Well executed.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 30, 2022

Emily The Criminal

#FF22 Emily The Criminal sees Aubrey Plaza go from petty criminal to something bigger, as her circumstances (and attitude) force her towards wider confrontation.

Fun, clear plotting, and a solid cast make this a relatively easy to watch drama.

Good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 30, 2022

The Innocents

#FF22 The Innocents is a Scandinavian horror about some very young kids with difficulties, who gain growing powers.

There are some very difficult to watch scenes in here and the last act drags, but there's a creeping dread throughout that makes this compelling.

Decent.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 30, 2022

Barbarian

#FF22 Barbarian is about a woman who finds her AirBnB is already occupied but quickly realises that's not her biggest problem.

The pacing here is excellent with several switches in context as we learn more about what's happening. Fantastic direction.

An excellent, modern horror

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 30, 2022

The October Winner

Some very good options but I’m going with Barbarian as the kind of horror I love: it’s inventive, and constantly shifting.

Film Fight 2022: September

Nine films for the September Film Fight!

Rogue Agent

#FF22 Rogue Agent starts with the worst kind of narration; all tell and now show.

It picks up significantly, as it moves into the main story of a charismatic con man. Arterton is excellent, as usual, and Norton makes the whole thing work.

Well paced, clear stakes, worth seeing.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) September 10, 2022

Three Thousand Years Of Longing

#FF22 Three Thousand Years Of Longing is intriguingly small, yet expansive.

Set mostly in a room with an academic and a Djinn going back and forward, hearing stories that might be true or a trick. Swinton and Elba lack chemistry, but it doesn't matter.

Enjoyable, but flawed.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) September 10, 2022

The Forgiven

#FF22 The Forgiven doesn't linger long on its precipitating event, quickly going to considerations and consequences.

The cast is strong, split between the cartoonishly rich and grounded poverty; the film builds on the juxtaposition.

Dark humour, drama, and tragedy. Very good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) September 10, 2022

Fall

#FF22 Fall is an okay story of two women being stuck 2000ft up a TV tower.

It doesn't earn its twists (which are telegraphed) and the budget shows through at times, but it does it's job of making you jump at just the right moment.

Okay, but lightweight.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) September 10, 2022

Bodies Bodies Bodies

#FF22 Bodies Bodies Bodies is a tight little slasher, that encapsulates the Werewolf/Among Us experience well.

Maybe a little predictable but fun. Doesn’t outstay it’s welcome.

Perfect popcorn fodder.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) September 11, 2022

See How They Run

#FF22 See How They Run tries to spoof Agatha Christie, but comes across as a little self-satisfied.

It all feels underwritten, with a great cast delivering lines flatly; failing to find the comedy in the farce. The structure and callbacks are painfully obvious.

Forgettable.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) September 24, 2022

Funny Pages

#FF22 Funny Pages sees a young cartoonist, obsessed with the likes of Robert Crumb, devolve his life into the kind of low-life world he sees in comics.

Like a Solondz film but without the bite, the plot sprawls but rarely progresses. A little twisted and funny, but also hollow.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) September 24, 2022

Crimes Of The Future

#FF22 Crimes Of The Future is Cronenberg returning to his body horror roots, with an eco-tinged story of squirming surgery as art.

Solid leads, but the story is muddled and meandering, with languid pacing.

Disturbing in places, leering in others. Uncomfortable, as is the point.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 2, 2022

I Came By

#FF22 I Came By is a horror thriller, billed as a home invasion but is actually something... different.

The four leads are excellent, taking their turn in the spotlight as the plot twists, and twist it does. It never quite works out how you expect; a rare thing these days.

Good

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 2, 2022

The September Winner

Not a bad month, but not a great one either; lots of solid films, but not much that’s great. I think the winner is The Forgiven for walking quite a difficult path and pulling it off.

Film Fight 2022: August

A short month with only 4 films…

Bullet Train

#FF22 Bullet Train is a fine if somewhat forgettable entry into the overstylized action genre.

People have codenames that appear onscreen, we jump around the timeline, the dialogue is snappy: you've seen this before.

Entertaining enough action. Generally fine.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 7, 2022

Prey

#FF22 Prey is an excellent entry in the Predator franchise.

Beautifully shot, well-paced, tense, brutal action. Midthunder is fantastic as the lead, bringing tenacity and vulnerability to the role.

Original and exciting, while bringing back the ethos of the original.

Must see.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 7, 2022

Nope

#FF22 Nope defies neat explanations and simple categorisation.

The cast are strong and it looks great, with the main action being clear and focussed. That recurring sequence in the TV studio is superbly tense. The message is harder to extract.

Enjoyable, but not straightforward

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 20, 2022

Day Shift

#FF22 Day Shift starts with a solid, quiet action scene that sets the world up neatly...

...and quickly devolves. While the action remains decent, the plot is nonsense and most of the characters don't make sense; world-stuffing the film for a franchise.

Forgettably entertaining

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 21, 2022

The August Winner

While Nope is interesting, Prey has to be the winner: a great action film that rejuvenates a flagging franchise.

Film Fight 2022: July

A very light and unimpressive month for July, with just three films.

Thor: Love And Thunder

#FF22 Thor: Love And Thunder is about as paint by numbers as Marvel gets.

It takes no chances, does nothing fresh. The plot and character development are woefully undercooked too: Bale is solid as Gorr, but it doesn't give him much time to build up. Some fun moments.

Meh.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 24, 2022

Where The Crawdads Sing

#FF22 Where The Crawdads Sing is comically bad.

The dialogue and performances are close to parody territory with only Strathairn any good. The plot is as silly as it is predictable. I'm not bothered about not seeing what actually happened, because I'd long stopped caring.

Avoid

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 24, 2022

The Gray Man

#FF22 The Gray Man doesn't do much with its strong cast.

The plot is straight from the action-film-script generator, with action that matches, and little in the way of originality or flair anywhere. Setup, location, set-piece, over and over, with little connection.

Skip.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 7, 2022

The July Winner

A pretty grim month, so Thor: Love And Thunder inexplicably wins by being the only film I might possibly watch a second time many years from now.

Film Fight 2022: June

A fairly short Film Fight for June, with just 5 films.

Men

#FF22 Men is not a subtle film in the slightest.

It is, however, an effective small horror, with exceptionally strong performances by the two leads, and several visceral and creepy moments that will live in my memory.

A little slow towards the end, but decent.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) June 12, 2022

Spiderhead

#FF22 Spiderhead takes a broad look at abuses in pharmaceuticals, focussing on issues of control like a lightweight Milgram experiment.

The cast are fine, seemingly having fun, and the runtime passes quickly, but the film doesn't have much to say in the end.

Okay.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 3, 2022

Pleasure

#FF22 Pleasure focusses on issues of consent and power in the adult entertainment industry.

Bella's journey is not an easy watch, each step taking her further into that world. Thyberg's direction/writing is even-handed: neither celebrating or condeming it.

Good, not easy.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 3, 2022

Top Gun: Maverick

#FF22 Top Gun: Maverick is very much an 80s film in modern clothing.

The plot is silly and the machismo is over the top, with too many nods to the original. But it's still a lot of fun. The action sequences are predictable, but spectacular, and it all comes together.

Fun.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 3, 2022

Hustle

#FF22 Hustle follows a basketball scout, looking for his next break.

This is a serious Sandler film, without being dour. It pulls together several underdog and sport movie plots into something new enough. There are laughs, difficulties, and triumphs that keep it moving.

Solid.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 3, 2022

The June Winner

A tricky one because there’s plenty of good but nothing excellent here. I think the movie I’d see again soonest is Men, so that’s the winner.
