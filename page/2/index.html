Happenings

Film Fight 2023: November

Seven films this month…

Fingernails

#FF23 Fingernails has an interesting premise (a test that claims to find perfect couple matches) but doesn't really deliver on it.

Riz Ahmed and Jesse Buckley are strong as always but the story doesn't go anywhere, with a flat and predictable conclusion.

Disappointing.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) December 2, 2023

Quiz Lady

#FF23 Quiz Lady is a surprisingly solid , easy going comedy about a quiz fanatic out of her depth.

The premise works well, the setups deliver, and both Awkwafina and Sandra Oh are excellent. With a great supporting cast, this works.

Worth seeing.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) December 2, 2023

The Killer

#FF23 The Killer sees David Finch take on the hitman genre, with good results.

Fassbender is the titular character, whose bored, clinical monologue we're stuck in - through a botched operation and its consequences. Well-paced but dry, with some nice smaller roles.

Good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) December 2, 2023

Dream Scenario

#FF23 Dream Scenario is delightfully bizarre: a boring man starts to appear in everyone's dreams, making him an overnight celebrity.

Cage is relatively subdued, but let's the petty jealousy and resentment bubble to the surface. It's fun, weird, and takes some turns.

Good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) December 2, 2023

The Royal Hotel

#FF23 The Royal Hotel sees two broke American women take a job in the middle of the Australian Outback.

The threat of harm looms over every scene, making for some very uncomfortable moments; and memorable characters. Garner is as great as always.

Very good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) December 2, 2023

Anatomy Of A Fall

#FF23 Anatomy Of A Fall is a stunning drama that begins small and personal, and spans into a courtroom.

It plays in the dynamics of a relationship that has suffered tragedy, and the lies people tell themselves to cope. The lead is cold, but is she a killer?

Excellent.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) December 2, 2023

The Marvels

#FF23 The Marvels shows that, while Marvel fatigue is very real, they can still do something fun.

The trio of lead characters work well together, bringing levity in certain places, and the weight of consequences elsewhere. The central mechanic allows for solid action.

Good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) December 2, 2023

The November Winner

I think The Royal Hotel would win most other months, but Anatomy Of A Fall is just a little better.

Film Fight 2023: October

Only six films this month in the Film Fight.

Talk To Me

#FF23 Talk To Me is an excellent modern horror.

The rules are laid out clearly, and the messiness of breaking them is clear. That one scene (you'll know which) was absolutely gruesome, but not gratuitous. Just well-handled the whole way through.

Very good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 8, 2023

The Caine Mutiny Court-Martial

#FF23 The Caine Mutiny Court-Martial is a tight, performance and plot focussed courtroom drama.

You expect a flashback that isn't coming: Friedkin's final film is about the truth as the witnesses see it, not what happened. Deftly paced and unveiled.

Excellent.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 8, 2023

The Creator

#FF23 The Creator is a composite of a bunch of stuff you've seen before.

It's not bad, maybe a little long, but there's nothing fresh here. Some of the action works well enough, and it's enjoyable as mindless fodder but you'll likely forget it pretty quickly.

Fine.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) November 4, 2023

You’re Killing Me

#FF23 You're Killing Me is a decent horror thriller about a young woman trying to escape a bad situation.

The middle section of the film is strung out a little long, and the ending is a little ridiculous, but it builds tension reasonably well. People largely do smart stuff

Okay

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) November 4, 2023

Totally Killer

#FF23 Totally Killer is a fun horror comedy that plays with sci-fi and horror tropes gleefully.

The blasé attitudes of the 80s are played for laughs, which works well. The plot is a little predictable, but fun enough. Shipka makes the whole thing come together.

Good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) November 4, 2023

Pain Hustlers

#FF23 Pain Hustlers is the story of the disastrous sales culture in US pharmaceuticals.

You can see where it's going from the outset, but it's a decent ride getting there. Blunt and Evans are both very good in their roles, bringing empathy and sleaze in the right amounts.

Good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) November 4, 2023

The October Winner

There’s really only one option: The Caine Mutiny Court-Martial, as a tight, well-performed drama.

Film Fight 2023: September

Eight films in Film Fight this month…

Meg 2: The Trench

#FF23 Meg 2: The Trench is a fairly flat sequel to the ridiculous original.

It spends a lot more time with the badly-written characters, and less time with big sharks eating things. For a Big Shark Eating Things movie, that's a real minus point.

Forgettable.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) September 10, 2023

The Equaliser 3

#FF23 The Equaliser 3 sees the franchise switch to an. Italian coastal town.

The action is good, but gets less screen time. The plot is fine but weirdly paced. Everything just kinda works without being particularly great. A perfectly fine action movie with decent moments.

Okay.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) September 10, 2023

Strays

#FF23 Strays is a fun comedy concept, following a dog out for revenge and finding friendship.

Some funny moments, but it goes back to the same well a little too often (I get it, dogs pee). Lightweight, but reasonably made.

Decent.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) September 10, 2023

Past Lives

#FF23 Past Lives is a melancholy look at missed opportunity and what could have been.

While the end drags a little, the pacing is otherwise great; lots of slow, deliberate steps. It's a pensive film, that doesn't try to take an easy route.

Very good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) September 30, 2023

No-One Will Save You

#FF23 No-One Will Save you is a bit of a mixed bag.

As a near-silent alien invasion film, it's damn near jaw-dropping, full of tension and excellent set pieces. When it tries to be a vague metaphor for trauma, it falls flat.

Worth seeing, but expect to roll you eyes.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) September 30, 2023

Dumb Money

#FF23 Dumb Money is a look at the Game Stop short squeeze.

It tries to manufacture tension by bringing in lots of perspectives, but fails to do anything interesting or examine the subject particularly deeply.

Fine but forgettable.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) September 30, 2023

Bottoms

#FF23 Bottoms is about two friends who create a fight club to try and hook up with their crushes.

It's funny, original, stylish, keeps moving fast, and hits an interesting tone. Everything is a little off in a way that just kinda makes sense. Solid performances too.

Excellent.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) September 30, 2023

Reptile

#FF23 Reptile is a difficult film to watch, about a murder and its investigation.

It's meanderingly paced and far too long, but there's something hypnotic about the tone. You'll see many of the twists coming but it's really more about the feel of the thing.

Okay.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) September 30, 2023

The September Winner

The winner is Bottoms for hitting the mark every single moment. Very good.

Film Fight 2023: August

A decent month with eight films…

The Beanie Bubble

#FF23 The Beanie Bubble is surprisingly enjoyable.

Galifianakis has never been better than as the engaging/manipulative company founder, and the rest of the cast is strong too (particularly Banks). An interesting story, maybe a little too neatly wrapped up, told well.

Good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 26, 2023

Joy Ride

#FF23 Joy Ride is a great comedy about friendship and find out who you are.

While the last act drags through Too Many Endings, there are solid laughs throughout, as well as some touching moments. Great cast, mostly well-paced.

Worth seeing.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 26, 2023

Heart Of Stone

#FF23 Heart Of Stone is straight form Netflix's content churn factory.

There are some good ideas and some solid set pieces, but a lot of hollow sheen and comic book set dressing. The opening sequence is the best part, so feel free to stop after that.

Meh.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 26, 2023

Blue Beetle

#FF23 Blue Beetle is Yet Another DC Film.

It's less impressed with itself than, say, Flash, with a likeable hero, generic story, and okay action. Nothing really feels like it matters. It's not terrible, it's just mindless popcorn fodder.

Fine for what it is.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 26, 2023

Corner Office

#FF23 Corner Office is an intriguing, off-beat film about an office worker who becomes his best self when he goes into a specific room.

Hamm plays against type as the awkward lead, getting his sense of self-delusion and self-importance just right. Slow but never dull.

Very good

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 26, 2023

The Blackening

#FF23 The Blackening is a horror satire in the vein of Scream, that explores tropes around Black people in horror movies.

It's amusing enough, has a couple of jump scares, and is well put together. The main cast all do a job of selling the idea with just enough of a wink

Decent

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) September 10, 2023

Inside

#FF23 Inside sees Willem Dafoe as an art thief trapped in a high-end apartment, trying desperately to escape.

Dafoe is excellent, growing more deranged as time goes on. The pacing drags in places, but it works through the premise methodically and in near-silence.

Very good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) September 10, 2023

Paint

#FF23 Paint is a bone-dry take on a Bob Ross-like public access painter, and his new rival.

Wilson is perfectly cast: the right mix of straight man, with a little goofiness. While it does pull on some of the threads a little long, it's a nice enough comedy.

Okay.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) September 10, 2023

The August Winner

A little torn between Inside and Corner Office, but Corner Office edges it - I’ve got a soft spot for weird workplace films.

Film Fight 2023: July

I honestly thought I posted this at the start of August, but here it is: 11 films in the July fight.

Blackberry

#FF23 Blackberry is a dramatisation of the rise and fall of RIM, and the Blackberry phone.

Baruchel is good, but Howerton has never been than as the sociopathic executive who gets them early success. The film drags a little towards the end, but is very solid up until then.

Good

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 30, 2023

Fool’s Paradise

#FF23 Fool's Paradise is about Hollywood and stardom.

While Charlie Day's physical performance is good, there's not enough of a plot or decent characterisation to make the film work. It feels cobbled together from a bunch of worse drafts. For a comedy there are few laughs.

Skip

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 30, 2023

Smoking Causes Coughing

#FF23 Smoking Causes Coughing is an odd, French comedy.

It treats it's over-the-top premise (a Power Rangers like group on a team bonding camping session), with seriousness, even as things get weirder. It slides from this into a bunch of side stories that are a mixed bunch

Odd.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 30, 2023

Mission Impossible: Dead Reckoning Part One

#FF23 Mission Impossible: Dead Reckoning Part One is the kind of big action we like to see in theatres

Bloated by having to explain the unusual (and eye-rolling) villain, when the set pieces kick off it's worth the wait. Cruise shows his trademark commitment to the stunts.

Good

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 30, 2023

The Flash

#FF23 The Flash has a handful of good ideas and fun action, squandered in a mess of a flabby film.

Keaton is great, but ultimately just in the movie as fan service (though not the most egregious). The visual effects are often terrible: like a bad Unreal engine demo.

Skip.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 30, 2023

How To Blow Up A Pipeline

#FF23 How To Blow Up A Pipeline is a smart, focussed, methodical film about... well, blowing up a pipeline.

The structure, going back and forth between character's back stories, works well; the pacing is tight without being frantic; and the story pans out nicely.

Very good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 30, 2023

Oppenheimer

#FF23 Oppenheimer is a surprisingly intimate film for Nolan, focussing more on the man himself than the wider action around him.

Murphy puts in a career-best performance, shining in an absolutely stacked cast. Downey Jr too. At three hours, it moves quickly.

Excellent.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 30, 2023

The Out-laws

#FF23 The Out-Laws is a fairly flimsy comedy about in-laws who are bank robbers.

There's a bunch of flab in the middle here, but it's fun if forgettable and lightweight. Brosnan seems to be having some fun at least.

Fine.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 30, 2023

They Cloned Tyrone

#FF23 They Cloned Tyrone is an odd mix: at times a broad, goofy adventure, at others a little darker, with clear nods to blaxploitation films, and an underlying message around race in America.

Boyega, Foxx, and Parris all put in good performances that make it work.

Very good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 30, 2023

The Artifice Girl

#FF23 The Artifice Girl is an excellent sci-fi indie about AI and trauma.

The second act is a little expositional, but the first and last acts of the three act structure are fantastic. Weighty ideas from which the director/write/star doesn't flinch.

An excellent feature debut.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 30, 2023

Barbie

#FF23 Barbie is an outrageously big take on the franchise.

Robbie and Gosling are perfectly cast with their completely bought-in performances; it could've been goofy in less capable hands. Funny, knowing, with a good message. Doesn't know how to end but has fun on the way

Great

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 30, 2023

The July Winner

Some very good films this month, but the winner for me is Oppenheimer; Nolan’s most interesting work in a long time.
