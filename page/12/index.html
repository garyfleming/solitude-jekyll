Happenings

Film Fight 2020: December

A decent number of films for December with 10 new films, but are they any good?

Mank

#FF20 Mank is an ode to classic cinema. It takes on the look, movement, audio etc, down to lens choices.

The cast are excellent, with Oldman and Seyfried the stand-outs.

The back-and-forth structure doesn't work for me, but I understand the why.

A solid movie.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) December 6, 2020

Inheritance

#FF20 Inheritance is not a good movie.

At every turn, the characters make bizarre choices, driven by handwaved issues, that simply do not make sense.

The performances are so-so, with British actors doing American accents that fall apart pretty consistently.

Skip.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) December 6, 2020

Ava

#FF20 Ava is a fairly forgettable film about an assassin who starts asking too many questions. A solid main cast doesn't help a cliched plot, especially with a dull family b-plot in the background.

A few okay action moments, but you can safely skip this.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) December 13, 2020

Archenemy

#FF20 Archenemy fails to deliver much beyond some mildly interesting visuals, a contrast between neon and grimy grain.

The story is predictable, and the dialogue dull. There's little to care about here, and a lot to dislike.

Avoid.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) December 20, 2020

Yes, God, Yes

#FF20 Yes, God, Yes is a fairly limp light comedy about a Christian high school retreat, and some of the hypocrisy around sex.

It doesn't have enough teeth to deliver a message, or enough laughs to be particularly entertaining.

Skip.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) December 20, 2020

Ma Rainey’s Black Bottom

#FF20 Ma Rainey's Black Bottom is a fantastic performance showcase for both Viola Davis and Chadwick Boseman; both very different, but captivating.

Some of the longer monologues feel a little out of place, but are well executed.

Very good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) December 27, 2020

The Midnight Sky

#FF20 The Midnight Sky gets more disappointing the longer it goes on. It begins beautifully, and slowly.

What should have been a quiet, contemplative movie about what we've done to the world and second chances, gives way to cliche and unnecessary contrivance.

Skip.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) December 27, 2020

Wonder Woman 1984

#FF20 Wonder Woman 1984 squanders a good cast on a mess of a film.

Overlong, dull, dumb, and badly paced. It's a real slog.

Even the action is uninspired, badly edited, and visually poor. Suprising, given the original.

There’s little to recommend here. Avoid.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) January 3, 2021

Greyhound

#FF20 Greyhound is an unusual film.

It doesn't have much of a defined structure: focussing on the constant but quiet action of this kind of naval combat.

It's quite human, despite most of the people being abstract figures on distant ships.

Grounded, steady, solid. Watchable.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) January 3, 2021

The 2nd

#FF20 The 2nd is one of the worst films I saw in 2020.

Nonsensical plot, bad action, bad acting; the works. None of it makes any sense, and it's not even particularly entertaining along the way.

Big avoid here.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) January 3, 2021

The December Winner

There was a surprising amount of bad stuff this month, but Ma Rainey’s Black Bottom is a more than deserving winner, for those excellent lead performances.

Film Fight 2020: November

For November’s Film Fight, we have 6 films.

Borat Subsequent Moviefilm

#FF20 Borat Subsequent Moviefilm is a bit of a tired take on Sascha Baron-Cohen's spoof characters. There's little here you haven't seen before, with empty shocks and janky setups; all merged together to form a "plot".

The laughs are few. This is a dull, dated film. Skip.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) November 15, 2020

His House

#FF20 His House is an excellent horror film, that blends the supernatural with the psychological threats of every day life for refugees. It plays with our expectations, while putting forward messages about integration.

An exceptional debut, great performances, and a solid horror

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) November 15, 2020

#Alive

#FF20 #Alive is an okay South Korean zombie film.

The characters and set-up are fine, but there's little new here. The social media framing doesn't go far enough to give a new spin.

A fine 90 minutes, but not groundbreaking.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) November 15, 2020

The King of Staten Island

#FF20 The King of Staten Island is a better comedy than I expected. Both Pete Davidson and Bill Burr deliver some great moments, grounded in some emotional weight.

Good pace, good length, but with one or two dead-ends.

Pretty good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) November 15, 2020

The Babysitter: Killer Queen

#FF20 The Babysitter: Killer Queen is very much a sequel to the first one, amped up.

Overstylised, ridiculous gore, over-the-top acting, and more silliness. The story is pretty flimsy, with a few set-ups for not very much pay-off.

Fine if you want something easy and dumb.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) November 15, 2020

Hillbilly Elegy

#FF20 Hillbilly Elegy fails to deliver a compelling narrative.

There’s the heart of a good story here, brought down by Hollywood schmaltz and cliched plot devices.

Glenn Close and Amy Adam’s put in fantastic performances, but Basso less so.

Good and bad parts. Flawed.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) November 29, 2020

The November Winner

The winner is His House, a must-see horror film.

Film Fight 2020: October

My concentration was elsewhere so only three films in October.

Enola Holmes

#FF20 Enola Holmes didn't work for me. While the cast are charming, the plot they're running through feels forced and overlong; a series of set pieces and encounters, flimsily connected. None of the characters earned their emotional beats.

Honestly, a bit dull.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 11, 2020

The Other Lamb

#FF20 The Other Lamb follows a cult member starting to become disillusioned with her leader.

This film is gorgeous: from the saturated colours against the natural, to some of the vistas. Stunning cinematogrpahy.

The plot is lacking. The changes and ending feel cliched. Decent.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 26, 2020

The Trial of The Chicago 7

#FF20 The Trial Of The Chicago 7 is a look at government power, protest, and counter-culture, through the lens of a courtroom drama.

Strong performances from an excellent cast, tight dialogue, and excellent structuring have the film going at a great pace.

Very good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 31, 2020

The October Winner

The winner for October is The Trial Of The Chicago 7, a timely reminder of systemic injustice.

Film Fight 2020: September

A reasonable month, with 6 new films…

Get Duked

#FF20 Get Duked is a decent dark comedy, that clearly takes inspiration from Hot Fuzz, both tonally and visually.

It's not quite as slick as Wright's work, but has some very funny moments, neat visuals, and a fun plot.

A solid feature debut. Good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) September 6, 2020

I’m Thinking of Ending Things

#FF20 I'm Thinking of Ending Things is a difficult movie.

The conversation at the start is excellent, and builds tension, warps into unease, and a creeping horror. The last act drops the ball, focussing on indulgence, not delivery.

Performances are all excellent.

Very mixed.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) September 6, 2020

Spree

#FF20 Spree is a fairly broad and somewhat satirical look at what streaming and influencer culture does to people, as we follow a guy who decides on a dangerous path forward.

Reasonably well shot, decent pacing, and a bit silly. It's all fairly predictable. Okay.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) September 27, 2020

The Devil All The Time

#FF20 The Devil All The Time is less than the sum of its parts.

A really good cast are mired in various plots we've seen before that tie together in a way that seems forced. It suffers from horrible narration: have the actors show me, don't tell me.

Pretty forgettable. Skip.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) September 27, 2020

Freaks: You’re One Of Us

#FF20 Freaks: You're One Of Us is a German superhero story about people whose powers have been suppressed.

It's fine, if entirely predictable and chock full of stereotypes. There are a few neat moments, but mostly this is just mild entertainment.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 3, 2020

Bill & Ted Face The Music

#FF20 Bill & Ted Face The Music is everything no-one needed. It brings back old characters with nods and winks, adds new ones that are uninteresting, and mostly tries to get by on nostalgia.

Twenty minutes in you'll know exactly what's going to happen.

Tired and dull.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) October 3, 2020

The September Winner

Turns out there wasn’t much worthwhile this month, so the winner is Get Duked.

Film Fight 2020: August

A little quieter than recent months, but still 7 new films.

7500

#FF20 7500 says Joseph Gordon-Levitt as a co-pilot fighting off a hijacking attempt.

A solid one location film, with great sound design, tense moments, and some neat plotting. The last 20-30 minutes drag a little, but it holds up overall.

Worth seeing.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 9, 2020

The Vast of Night

#FF20 The Vast of Night never quite reaches where it should.

The performances are fine with some very neat shots (and one that is a bit too clever), but the script mistakes running frantically between places with action. Much of the story is flat and expositional.

Meh.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 9, 2020

Project Power

#FF20 Project Power is a bit of a mess.

Despite a solid cast, it's a bit of a mess. The script feels underwritten, bumbling from set piece to set piece in a clunky way.

The action itself is bad: a mess of edits that makes it difficult to enjoy any of it.

Really not good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 16, 2020

Days of the Bagnold Summer

#FF20 Days of the Bagnold Summer is a charming story about a single mum and her metalhead son.

It perfectly plays teenage grump and awkwardness against a librarian in cardigan. The neat cinematography provides lots of beautiful shots and framing.

Funny, sweet, and worth seeing.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 22, 2020

An American Pickle

#FF20 An American Pickle is a fish out of water buddy comedy, about a man preserved in brine for 100 years.

It has moments of humour, but is ultimately a bit one note. Neither of Seth Rogan's characters are particularly 2-dimensional.

Meh.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 22, 2020

Never Rarely Sometimes Always

#FF20 Never Rarely Sometimes Always is an important look at how difficult it can be to get an abortion in the US.

Shot in a grounded style, the quietness amplifies the reality being shown. The performances are equally subtle.

Sometimes difficult viewing, but worth seeing.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 31, 2020

Tenet

#FF29 Tenet is a Nolan film, through and through.

Spectacular action and a complicated narrative, with a great performance by Washington. Also suffers from being excessively expositional, and having terrible sound design, as with most of his films.

Worth seeing at least once.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 31, 2020

The August Winner

The winner for August is Never Rarely Sometimes Always, a difficult but important watch, that’s well put together.
