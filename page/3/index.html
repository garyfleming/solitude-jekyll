Film Fight 2024: April

Very late but only two films for April…

Monkey Man

#FF24 Monkey Man is an interesting entry in the Wick-inspired action genre.

Dev Patel absolutely looks the part and has the presence to pull this off. There are a bunch of very good action scenes, and some where Patel's direction is too wobbly and edited.

Worth seeing.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) April 14, 2024

Civil War

#FF24 Civil War is gripping for its entire runtime.

The story is a little more compressed than I'd like, but the performances are excellent; Spaeny is going places. There are some shot choices I want to pour over on a second viewing, but the first was more than enough

Excellent

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) April 14, 2024

The April Winner

They’re both good but the winner is Civil War.

Film Fight 2024: March

Bit late, but there are six films in the fight this month

Ricky Stanicky

#FF24 Ricky Stanicky is a farcical comedy about an imaginary friend who kind becomes real.

Cena and Efron are as dependable as always. The film is kinda sweet in places, if a little obvious. Not a masterpiece but perfectly watchable.

Good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) April 14, 2024

Drive Away Dolls

#FF24 Drive-Away Dolls is a comedy that is less than the sum of its parts.

A good cast, a great director, a fine premise - just not many laughs. It's got moments, but there's not enough for the runtime. Not bad just not particularly interesting.

Okay.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) April 14, 2024

Dune Part 2

#FF24 Dune part 2 is the epic spectacle that it need to be.

The worm rider looks terrible, but everything else is incredible - the soundtrack is massive, the story feels grand and just alien enough, the performances work. An experience like little else.

Excellent.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) April 14, 2024

Argylle

#FF24 Argylle feels like it missteps on almost every level.

Rockwell and Cavill come out largely unscathed, but the rest of the cast seem confused or bored. The twists are forgettable. The action sequences are visually awful. And the plot? There isn't really a plot.

Avoid.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) April 14, 2024

Road House

#FF24 Road House is a cheap remake.

Gyllenhall is the only thing they’ve put money into, and he's solid. Macgregor is atrocious but looks great in the fights.

There are some genuinely excellent line deliveries but the whole thing is underwritten, and looks cheap.

Skip.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) April 14, 2024

Shirley

#FF24 Shirley tells the story of the first black woman to attempt a presidential bid.

It does a good job of showing the difficulties the titular character faces, but not as much showing why we should care about her specifically. A little dry at times but decent.

Watchable.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) April 14, 2024

The March Winner

The winner is Dune Part 2 and it’s not close. I’d been left a bit cold by part 1 when I first saw it, but warmed to it on second viewing - part 2 just worked for me.

Film Fight 2024: February

An excellent month with 6 movies

Self Reliance

#FF24 Self Reliance is an interesting, and slightly odd, dark comedy about a man who volunteers to be hunted for money.

The film keeps you guessing about a number of aspects of the game that is being played. Johnson and Kendrick keep you engaged throughout.

Good comedy.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) February 25, 2024

American Fiction

#FF24 American Fiction is an excellent look at society's expectations of race and the prejudice therein.

Wright is excellent, playing comedy and drama equally well, through very light and dark moments. As well as the more overt statements, it has a rich subtext too.

Very good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) February 25, 2024

Suncoast

#FF24 Suncoast is a family drama about a girl whose brother is terminally ill.

It doesn't always find the right tone, and much is about her trying to live her life, rather than a tightly plotted drama - it moves at an okay pace. Linney is excellent as the mother.

Okay.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) February 25, 2024

The Iron Claw

#FF24 The Iron Claw looks at the real wrestling family who faced tragedy after tragedy.

The entire cast sell the film incredibly well: physically and in delivery, both in and out of the ring; this could be Efron's best work. One ill-judged dream sequence aside, it's very good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) February 25, 2024

The Zone Of Interest

#FF24 The Zone Of Interest is a powerful look at the Nazis living next door to Auschwitz.

Much of the film is relatively (and intentionally) mundane; dealing with petty family moments, party politics, and life. But the dark underbelly is there and disturbing. Not easy

Excellent

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) February 25, 2024

Wicked Little Letters

#FF24 Wicked Little Letters is amusing, as it looks at how class and prejudice affected a real case of poison letters.

Colman/Buckley are strong and keep the film moving. The plot, though, is pretty predictable. The letters are mostly too chaste to be consistently comedic.

Fine

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) February 25, 2024

The February Winner

A very tough one with three movies that could win most months, but I think it’s American Fiction for me; so much more than the trailers showed.

Film Fight 2024: January

Very late, but 10 whole films in the January fight…

Freelance

#FF24 Freelance is an entirely forgettable and slightly ridiculous action film.

Alison Brie is great as always, and John Cena does fine. Clearly on a tight budget for the level of action they wanted, some scenes show that - and a couple look pretty good.

Not bad.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) January 20, 2024

Priscilla

#FF24 Priscilla is as shocking as it is beautiful.

Cailee Spaeny is perfectly cast as the young lead, through a world far too old for her; marrying a start who is cruel and controlling. The slightly dreamy look lends an otherworldliness to awful moments. Well-paced.

Very good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) January 20, 2024

Next Goal Wins

#FF24 Next Goal Wins is fun but formulaic.

You've seen the beats before: a bad team gains a grumpy coach, and they help each other become better. It's underwritten and obvious, but a perfectly fine film that's reasonably put together. You won't hate it.

Decent.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) January 20, 2024

The Holdovers

#FF24 The Holdovers finds a specific tone, and executes it well.

Giamatti's grumpy, better than thou professor plays perfectly against the troubled but brilliant boy he's asked to look after. Witty dialogue and fun moments, backed by some heartwarming scenes.

Very good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) January 20, 2024

Society Of The Snow

#FF24 Society Of The Snow tells the horrific story of Uruguayan rugby players lost in the Andes.

From the bone-crunching plane crash to the inevitable avalanche, the film doesn't revel in suffering but doesn't back away from it either. Difficult, but powerul.

Very worth seeing

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) January 20, 2024

Poor Things

#FF24 Poor Things sees Lanthimos embrace the weird in a search for humanity.

The main cast are strong, with Stone putting in a brilliant physical performance. The production deisgn is beautiful, but the wide lenses are intrusive to the point of distraction.

Funny, dark, great.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) January 20, 2024

Role Play

#FF24 Role Play is a passable action comedy, with some great actors (Cuoco, Oyelowo).

However, the fun, early ideas (like Nighy's extended sequence) get squandered by a generic, action-focussed last act. Easy going, at least, and doesn't overstay its welcome.

Okay.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) January 20, 2024

Lift

#FF24 Lift is the most generic of modern heist movies.

It has some incredible and utterly wasted talent (Mbatha-Raw, D'Onofrio) trod through a plot that could be AI generated, with a charmless lead. The action is messy, with far too many cuts.

Boring.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) January 20, 2024

The End We Start From

#FF24 The End We Start from is a look at motherhood through the lens of an ecological disaster.

The core performances (Comer in particular) are strong, especially as the situation grows more dire. The cameos feel more distracting than useful, but the story is solid enough.

Good

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) February 25, 2024

The Kitchen

#FF24 The Kitchen is about capitalism displacing those most in need.

The themes are well-worn, but it's reasonably well done, especially within a constrained budget. Kano is good as a reluctant father figure, with the relationship never getting cheesey.

Decent.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) February 25, 2024

The January Winner

Some very good contenders but the winner is Priscilla, a beautiful and disturbing look at celebrity.

Film Fight 2023: Finale

It was a bit more of an uneven year. There were some great films, but fewer in contention than previous years. I also was quite busy so saw fewer films in the cinema

As per the caveat every year, Film Fight is done in a (non particularly serious) knock-out style: it can pick my favourite film of the year, but nothing else. (Also the very idea of a single favourite film is a bit silly.)

The honorable mentions: films that weren’t good enough to win their own month, but still worth seeing.

And now the monthly winners:

  • January: Tár
  • February: All Quiet On The Western Front
  • March: Women talking
  • April: Tetris
  • May: Beau is Afraid
  • June: Alice, Darling
  • July: Oppenheimer
  • August: Corner Office
  • September: Bottoms
  • October: The Caine Mutiny Court-Martial
  • November: Anatomy Of A Fall
  • December: Maestro

From that, there’s a lot of good stuff to choose from and you’re not going to go wrong with any of it (except maybe Tetris).

We have great but very different courtroom dramas in both The Caine Mutiny Court-Martial and Anatomy Of A Fall, the former being antagonistic and the latter being about what gets mispoken in an argument.

Elsewhere, Tár and Maestro paint very different pictures of obsessive genius, and desire leading to conflict.

I think Bottoms is the first comedy to make it through in a while, but is well deserved - it made me laught more than I thought it would.

We have Women Talking and Alice, Darling showing the consequences of horrific abuse: quietly, then very loudly. Both have stayed with me.

As always, I’m a little torn as to what my favourite film was but I think I’m going to go with Tár - mixing an incredible central performance, with some directing that was stunning.
