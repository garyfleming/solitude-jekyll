Film Fight 2023: June

A short month with only five films in the fight.

Alice, Darling

#FF23 Alice, Darling looks at abusive relationships in a more complex way than most.

Kendrick is excellent as the lead, but the whole cast shines. The script drips out what's been happening, while showing how decent people can get trapped by abusers.

Excellent but difficult.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) June 25, 2023

Extraction 2

#FF23 Extraction 2 is an excellent follow-up to the original.

It once again focusses on one massive action set-piece shot to look like a oner. It's ridiculous, but fun. The back-end of the film drags, but there's enough silly action to get through it.

Good fun.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) June 25, 2023

Spider-Man: Across The Spider-Verse

#FF23 Spider-Man: Across the Spider-Verse is a disappointment.

It's visually interesting but not much else works. It's far too long, with shoe-horned character arcs, and a bunch of fan service. As it's the first of two parts, it doesn't have a conclusion.

Fairly boring.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) June 25, 2023

No Hard Feelings

#FF23 No Hard Feelings will hardly make you feel.

It has some amusing moments throughout, but is mostly a little flat. The premise doesn't work well enough to setup the various incidents, and the emotional moments simply don't work.

It's not awful, but it's not great.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) June 25, 2023

Asteroid City

#FF23 Asteroid City is the usual mixture of things you expect from Wes Anderson.

Precise framing, pastel colours, dry but funny dialogue, a weird story with some interesting narrative choices, fun contrivances, and a self-seriousness undercut by low stakes.

His best in a while.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 1, 2023

The June Winner

Some good options, but the winner is Alice, Darling; an excellent, if difficult, watch.

Missing

#FF23 Missing is a thriller about a missing mother, seen through the lens of technology.

While there is some silliness in the tech, it's not awful for once. Predictable in the end, but tense enough to be enjoyable. Good performances.

Surprisingly solid.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) May 8, 2023

Guardians Of The Galaxy, Vol. 3

#FF23 Guardians Of The Galaxy, Vol. 3 is, as with everything Marvel these days, entirely what you expect.

It's big, silly, loud. There's some decent action and some that is a CG mess. Rocket's backstory is great, but sad. The film is at its worst when Pratt takes the lead.

Fine

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) May 8, 2023

Scream VI

#FF23 Scream VI manages to bring some fresh air to the creaking franchise.

Moving from a small town to a big city works surprisingly well. The opening rug pull is a neat change to the formula. Ortega is underutilised, and doesn't seem to want to be there.

Good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) May 8, 2023

The Mother

#FF23 The Mother is a dour, lower budget action film.

The action itself is edited to pieces, poorly hiding a lot of nonsense in the cuts. Given the plot isn't up to much either, this was a slot.

Skip.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) May 14, 2023

Beau Is Afraid

#FF23 Beau Is Afraid is a mixed bag.

Aster shows his control of the camera and mood remains masterful in its uneasy precision. The plot is entertaining and perplexing; at times demanding your attention, and at others boring. Overlong, but arresting.

Hard to love, easy to like.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) May 21, 2023

Dead Shot

#FF23 Dead Shot has promise as both as serious drama and a more action film, but it squanders both.

There are so many moments of cliched dialogue it's hard not to roll your eyes. The film looks fine, and the performances are okay, but nothing really rises up here.

Passable.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) May 29, 2023

Hypnotic

#FF23 Hypnotic is a conundrum. One one hand it's trying to be a solid, Nolan-esque, mindbender. One the other, it's plain silly.

You'll see a bunch of the twists coming but, as pure popcorn fodder, it doesn't matter too much. Some good ideas, that become goofy by the end.

Fun.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) May 29, 2023

The May Winner

While Missing almost had it, Beau Is Afraid takes it because it has stayed with me more.

Film Fight 2023: April

Tetris

#FF23 Tetris is a decent, if somewhat compressed take, on the legal drama and backstabbing to get the rights for the game.

The cast are fine and the direction functional enough, without being interesting. It's a little too neat in places, but a perfectly fine watch.

Okay.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) April 10, 2023

Air

#FF23 Air tells the story of the marketing drama behind getting Michael Jordan to sign with Nike.

The cast are fine, but everyone is well within their comfort zone. Similarly, Affleck as director turns in a solid enough movie, with nothing particularly flashy on camera.

Fine.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) April 10, 2023

Operation Fortune

#FF23 Operation Fortune is a silly, slightly under budget action film.

There are some decent sequences and big, silly performances (and some that don't quite work). The plotting is a bit of a mess to get between set pieces, but that's fine.

Forgettable but fun.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) April 10, 2023

Shazam: Fury Of The Gods

#FF23 Shazam: Fury Of The Gods is a weaker continuation of the original.

There's no subtext (Billy's feelings are regularly stated), uneven pacing, and an eye-rolling ending made of pure fan service.

The action is fun enough, there are a few funny moments. Popcorn fodder.

Meh.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) April 16, 2023

Assassin Club

#FF23 Assassin Club is a slog of a film.

The dialogue is absolutely leaden, the action is badly cut so as to be illegible, and the plot makes no sense. Noomi Rapace appears to be in a different movie. Sam Neill deserves better.

An action film with bad action is one to skip.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) April 16, 2023

Ghosted

#FF23 Ghosted is a light-hearted spy film, with a nonsense plot that is pretty poorly cobbled together.

There are repeated jokes about the tension between the two leads, but there is none. The music cues are genuinely terrible

There are worse things to watch but it's meh to bad

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) May 8, 2023

Film Fight 2023: March

Just six films in the fight this monthâ€¦

Creed 3

#FF23 Creed 3 is typical of the franchise: the ups and downs of fighting someone stronger, rising to the challenge, and succeeding in some way.

The cast are all solid, the plot is predictable, and everything is watchable. The visual flair in the last act is cringeworthy.

Fine.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) March 12, 2023

65

#FF23 65 treats it's somewhat ridiculous premise (space man crash lands on a dinosaur filled Earth) with an even tone: serious without being dour, not as a gag.

The runtime feels right, and Driver holds it together well enough. You'll see every twist coming but won't mind.

Okay

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) March 12, 2023

The Boston Strangler

#FF23 The Boston Strangler is a reasonable enough take on the reporting around the eponymous killer.

Both Knightley and Coon are solid as the journalists tracking down leads. It looks fine, but very generic: tealy/orange-tinged period drama.

Nothing special, but decent enough.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) March 19, 2023

John Wick: Chapter 4

#FF23 John Wick: Chapter 4 is exactly what you want in an action film.

It's a little too long, but is otherwise excellent: some of the best action and stunts in cinema, shot clearly and inventively, with a bunch of performers showing off what they can do.

Really good fun.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) April 2, 2023

Infinity Pool

#FF23 Infinity Pool is creeping body horror with something to say about predatorial tourism.

Goth and SkarsgÃ¥rd are both fully committed to their weird roles, delivering an unease that slowly ramps up. The pacing is a bit off and it doesn't get as weird as it might.

Pretty good

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) April 2, 2023

Women Talking

#FF23 Women Talking is disarmingly calm, for the most part, despite the horrific impetus.

A quiet conversation for 90 or so minutes, with some excellent performances from the entire cast. Despite the one major location, it remains grounded and never feels stagey.

Very good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) April 2, 2023

The March Winner

Some decent films in March, but the best film here is clearly Women Talking.

Film Fight 2023: February

Seven films in the February Film Fight.

The Whale

#FF23 The Whale is a real mix.

Fraser is excellent in a layered performance, that is empathetic. The rest of the characters are underwritten to the point of being one note, or have bizarrely, contrived arcs. Works well in moments, but falls apart as a whole.

Meh.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) February 5, 2023

Bank of Dave

#FF23 Bank Of Dave is about a man trying to create a bank to help working class people.

While the fish-out-of-water jokes are tired and the romantic subplot forced, the whole thing works as a feel-good, light, underdog story. It won't win any awards, but it's easy and solid.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) February 13, 2023

All Quiet On The Western Front

#FF23 All Quiet On The Western Front is a protracted look at the horrors of war, and the loss of innocence.

It treads no new ground, but it covers the ground it does spectacularly and devastatingly. It makes trench warfare personal and tense, with some horrifying moments.

Great

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) February 13, 2023

Knock At The Cabin

#FF23 Knock At The Cabin sees Shyamalan back on form with a small, contained horror thriller that lingers in psychological doubts.

Batista is excellent as a gentle but terrifying force, delivering an apocalyptic premise. The end is heavy-handed, but the rest works well.

Good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) February 13, 2023

Ant Man & The Wasp: Quantumania

#FF23 Ant Man & The Wasp: Quantumania is the blandest Marvel film in a while.

Jonathan Majors is great in his role, but the film itself is all world-building and no interesting plot or ideas. It lacks the humour and originality of the previous films.

Boring and disappointing.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) February 26, 2023

Sharper

#FF23 Sharper does so well for a lot of its runtime, and then squanders it in the ending.

You'll see most of the cons coming, but they're executed well. The cast are excellent and make it work, but the last act requires them to become very stupid. Doesn't work.

Okay.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) February 26, 2023

Cocaine bear

#FF23 Cocaine Bear is everything I expected it to be.

The title does most of the work: a comedic horror about a black bear finding quite a lot of coke and going on a rampage. It's over-the-top, fun, a bit silly, and a bit gory. Easy watching.

Enjoyable.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) February 26, 2023

The February Winner

While Cocaine Bear nearly got it for itâ€™s simple entertainment value, I think the winner has to be All Quiet On The Western Front, a difficult but powerful watch.
