Happenings

Film Fight 2021: July

For July, there are 10 films in the fight.

Freaky

#FF21 Freaky is an okay body-switch horror/comedy.

Vince Vaughn absolutely nails his performance, as a teenage girl stuck in a serial killer body.

Everything else feels a bit by-numbers, and looks a little cheap.

Easy watching, but not great.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 4, 2021

Minari

#FF21 Minari is an excellent film, about struggling to better your life, and failing to communicate with your partner.

Stuffed with so many good performances, Yuen stepping out is even more of an achievement.

Beautiful, thoughtful, sad, desperate, and joyful.

A masterpiece.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 4, 2021

The Tomorrow War

#FF21 The Tomorrow War is terrible.

Poor effects, generic art direction, tealy-orange grading, bad acting (except Strahovski and Simmons), muddled action sequences, no narrative coherence, cliched motivations. I could go on.

There is little redeemable here. Avoid.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 4, 2021

Body Brokers

#FF21 Body Brokers is a decent story about the business of addiction treatment in the US; and how its not always set-up to help.

Overbearing narration aside, the film works well: good performances, clear plot, maybe a little obvious.

Worth seeing.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 11, 2021

Honest Thief

#FF21 Honest Thief is yet another entirely forgettable bit of Liam Neeson fodder.

Clearly made on a budget, with bland action, a nonsense plot, and the constant reminder that character quirks are not characterisation.

Skip.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 11, 2021

Black Widow

#FF21 Black Widow is pretty uneven.

For every decent action sequence there’s one with janky editing or CG, for every good performance there’s a bad accent, funny moments meet tonal mismatches.

Plot is a mess. A mid-lower tier Marvel, for sure.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 11, 2021

Clemency

#FF21 Clemency is a powerful look at the death penalty and the toll it takes on the humanity of all involved parties.

The lead performances from Woodard and Hodge are stunning. Direction and pacing pull you through a difficult situation.

Affecting, powerful, tragic. Must see.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 1, 2021

First Cow

#FF21 First Cow is a fairly unique film.

Slow, and gentle; initially about friendship in a fairly uncaring frontier. It shifts slowly into something else, where risks are taken because it's the only way out.

Restrained, quiet, and intriguing. Worth seeing.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 1, 2021

Gunpowder Milkshake

#FF21 Gunpowder Milkshake does better than most knock-offs at capturing the essence of John Wick.

The action is decent and clearly shot, though not as polished. It's in the world building it does best, with clear stakes; all shot through with saturated neon colours.

Good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 1, 2021

The Suicide Squad

#FF21 The Suicide Squad is certainly an improvement on Suicide Squad.

Less dour, with some of DCs weirder characters left to be unashamedly different. Fairly funny for the most part. Some of the action works, some doesn't, and the pacing struggles towards the end.

Fine.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) August 1, 2021

The July Winner

The winner for July is Clemency, a film that’ll be hard to watch again.

Film Fight 2021: June

For the June Film Fight, we have 7 new(ish) films.

The Father

#FF21 The Father is an excellent film.

The performances are all great, but Hopkins going between charming, and confused, and angry in a moment is something to behold.

The narrative arc holds together, the set design is fantastic, and the cinematogrpahy is beautiful.

Must see.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) June 20, 2021

Shiva Baby

#FF21 Shiva Baby is a dark, personal comedy, focussing on an uncomfortable situation at a shiva.

What could descend into farce is carefully managed, drawing humour from the awkward rather than the ridiculous.

Well shot and performed, a solid directorial debut.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) June 20, 2021

Awake

#FF21 Awake is about a collapsing society due to people not being able to sleep.

It rushes the start of the strange behaviour, but long takes in clearly controlled spaces make for well done action sequences.w

Predictable but fun. Solid late-night fodder.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) June 20, 2021

Mogul Mowgli

#FF21 Mogul Mowgli sees Riz Ahmed as a rapper, who becomes increasingly ill on the verge of his break.

The tone and visuals shift from grounded to surreal to poetic; through an exploration of identity, memory, and family.

Strong lead performance, and direction.

Must see.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 4, 2021

Little Joe

#FF21 Little Joe is the quiet, calm, horror of slowly losing your identity and reality to something else.

Striking use of colour and framing play against very understated performances, and cold characters.

A little slow but worth seeing.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 4, 2021

A Quiet Place Part 2

#FF21 A Quiet Place Part 2 is... fine.

It doesn't add anything new to the world of the first film. It's a bunch of set pieces tied together in a reasonable enough way; and some of those work better than others.

An okay, brief, popcorn movie.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 4, 2021

Parallel

#FF21 Parallel is pretty standard time-travel/multiverse fare. The characters set out to exploit their new ability, learning that their actions have serious consequences.

While some of the effects are janky, the film is competent but not exceptional.

Worth a late night view.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) July 4, 2021

The June Winner

There are several strong candidates here, but I think it has to be The Father. Strong performances and direction, good concept, and left me in tears.

Film Fight 2021: May

Fairly big month, with 12 films for May.

Nomadland

#FF21 Nomadland is a masterpiece of a film.

The quiet, determined acceptance of a new life in McDormand's performance, the (real) people she meets stories interwoven into the narrative, stunning shot after stunning shot. It's a slice of reality and a slice of art.

Excellent.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) May 12, 2021

Stowaway

#FF21 Stowaway is fine space fodder.

The central performances are all good, and the visuals match expectations. It doesn't do much that's new: if you've seen a space disaster film, you've seen much of this.

Enjoyable, but not novel.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) May 12, 2021

The Mauritanian

#FF21 The Mauritanian sees a legal battle around the first trials at Guantanamo.

Cumberbatch and Foster aptly play the rival lawyers in a "Did he do it case?" that devolves into legal principle.

A little dry in places, but decent.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) May 12, 2021

Cherry

#FF21 Cherry is a story that could apply to many with few prospects: from the army into medication into drug abuse.

Stylish but humanising, with flurries of flights of fancy grounded by deeds. Lots of neat shots keep the visual interest up, but distract a little.

Very good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) May 12, 2021

Mortal Kombat

#FF21 Mortal Kombat is a mess of a film.

Outside of a few comically gruesome moments, nothing works. It's poorly acted and expositional. Most of the fights don't work, and many of the effects are laughably bad.

Skip.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) May 12, 2021

Sound of Metal

#FF21 Sound of Metal is a difficult film about getting to be comfortable with yourself.

The sound design and subtitles work well: excluding you when the lead is, and pulling you in later.

Riz Ahmed is fantastic, playing the part with justified anger, and self pity.

Must see.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) May 12, 2021

Boss Level

#FF21 Boss Level is a silly, time-loop-set action movie. It's over the top, not particularly clever, but quite fun in places.

Some of the set-pieces work quite nicely, but the cast is a bit overstuffed. Fewer characters would have been better.

Not great but it is entertaining.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) May 15, 2021

Without Remorse

#FF21 Without Remorse is what you'd expect from a Clancy action film: vague politics, a wronged hero, and a bad guy with a motive that doesn't really make sense.

The action sequences, particularly the apartment building shoot-out, are very well done but that's about it.

Meh.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) May 15, 2021

The Woman In The Window

#FF21 The Woman In The Window isn't a subtle film: from the plot to the edits to the camera angles, this is pure Hitchcock.

A melodrama about isolation, mental health, and murder, it's both brilliant and ridiculous. Adams and Oldman take some big swings; fun to watch.

Good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) May 15, 2021

Shirley

#FF21 Shirley is a fairly slow burn drama, with some great moments.

Elisabeth Moss is excellent, as always, and the rest of the cast do well to keep up. Everything behind the camera is fine too. It flounders with pacing: a bit languid, with few surprises in the plot.

Solid.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) June 6, 2021

Oxygen

#FF21 Oxygen is a good sci-fi thriller. A woman wakes in a medical pod that is losing air, with only an AI to help her try to figure out how to survive.

Great lead performance. You'll see much of what happens coming, but there's always something else right behind it.

Worth it.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) June 6, 2021

Army Of The Dead

#FF21 Army Of The Dead is pretty bad.

I'll happily suspend much disbelief for action films, but basically nothing in this film is coherent. A mess of bad dialogue, terrible plot, muddled visuals, bad music cues, and poorly done action.

A failure on every level.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) June 6, 2021

The May Winner

Some pretty solid films this month but the clear winner for me is Nomadland, a beautifully shot, fantastically performed masterpiece.

Film Fight 2021: April

For April’s Film Fight, we have 9 films.

Concrete Cowboy

#FF21 Concrete Cowboy makes a decent job of well-worn material (troubled teen sent to live in fish out of water place).

There are some threads that are very underdeveloped that should've been excised, but the main thrust is fine.

An okay film.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) April 4, 2021

Chaos Walking

#FF21 Chaos Walking is a bit of a mess. There really isn't any character development, the action is boring, and the main theme doesn't work particularly well.

There are moments where it looks like they'll do something interesting with the Noise conceit, but nope.

Skip.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) April 4, 2021

News of the World

#FF21 News Of The World is... fine. Hanks is as reliable as always, and the Helena Zengel puts in an excellent performance.

It looks decent enough, it's got some moments, but is entirely predictable. There's absolutely nothing new being offered here.

Okay.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) April 11, 2021

The Map Of Tiny Perfect Things

#FF21 The Map Of Tiny Perfect Things is a fun teen romance about two kids caught in a time loop in their small town.

The leads are charming, the loops work well, and it all builds up to a schmaltzy, predictable, but pleasant ending.

Solid.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) April 11, 2021

Run

#FF21 Run is an okay, if silly and cliched horror.

It's got moments of tension, despite the predictability, and both leads turn in good performances.

Nothing special but not bad. Pretty average.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) April 18, 2021

Promising Young Woman

#FF21 Promising Young Woman is excellent; snapping between revenge horror and sweet romance.

The music and visuals are great together; sharp, saturated, tightly edited.

But it's the lead performance that sells it.

The very end is too neat, but it doesn't matter. Must see.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) April 18, 2021

Nobody

#FF21 Nobody is an okay, cut-rate John Wick knock-off.

Odenkirk is good, but doesn't get enough to work with. Most of the action isn't on-par with what it's trying to copy, but the pacing keeps thinks moving forward anyway.

Fine but forgettable.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) April 18, 2021

Proxima

#FF21 Proxima is a slow, deliberate take on motherhood, from the perspective of an astronaut preparing for a year away.

Eva Green handles the mixture of physical and emotional challenge with the evenness expected of her exceptionally well.

A little thin in places, but worth it.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) April 18, 2021

Thunder Force

#FF21 Thunder Force is dull almost from start to finish, which ain't great for a comedy.

It wastes Octavia Spencer throughout, with McCarthy going through twice reheated schtick. Scenes with Bateman as a crab-man are the only ones with laughs.

Avoid.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) April 18, 2021

The April Winner

An easy one: Promising Young Woman has the story and style to take it here, and a better than average shot at film of the year.

Film Fight 2021: March

For March’s Film Fight we have 9 films.

Coming 2 America

#FF21 Coming 2 America is... fine. It does nothing new or interesting, spending too long falling into the revisiting all of the same characters rather than establish much that is new.

The dated elements are, thankfully, updated here.

A few nice moments, but nothing spectacular.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) March 7, 2021

Moxie

#FF21 Moxie is a nice mix of rebelling, getting things a bit wrong, and trying harder. There are moments that are a bit earnest and obvious, and others that hit pretty hard.

It's wrapped in a great soundtrack, is directed with a solid pace, and sets a decent tone.

Very good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) March 21, 2021

Barb & Star Go To Vista Del Mar

#FF21 Barb & Star Go To Vista Del Mar is surprisingly excellent.

It's tone veers from a little odd to very weird; it reminds me a lot of Anchorman, in the best way.

The cast are excellent, the plot is fine but doesn't matter, and I laughed a lot.

Watch this film.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) March 21, 2021

Kajillionaire

#FF21 Kajillionaire is an offbeat film about a grifter family, and what they put their daughter through.

Evan Rachel Woods is like you've never seen here before, nailing the socially awkward and downtrodden lead who begins to glimpse a better life.

A little slow, but solid.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) March 21, 2021

Dead Pigs

#FF21 Dead Pigs appears at first to be a series of vignettes about commercialism in China, but it's not long before the pieces start to interlock.

The direction here is incredible: clear, dynamic, beautifully light, frequently picking slightly longer shots.

Very good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) March 21, 2021

Judas And The Black Messiah

#FF21 Judas And The Black Messiah tells the story of Fred Hampton and Bill O'Neal incredibly.

The lead performances are great; empathetic and tragic at the same time. Looks great too; the opening section had me grinning with some subtle choices.

A must see.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) March 21, 2021

Fire Will Come

#FF21 Fire Will Come is the story of a released arsonist in rural Spain.

It's beautiful, in colour and composition, but also incredibly slow. We don't see his character develop or learn much about him.

I'm fine with an ambiguous ending, but make me care before we get there. Meh

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) March 28, 2021

Bad Trip

#FF21 Bad Trip takes the prank-reality-meets-story concept from Bad Grandpa and applies it to a road trip/romantic comedy.

It's not amazing. There are funny moments, but the reliance on shock and gross-out humour pulls this film down. It's all been done before.

Skip.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) March 28, 2021

Bacurau

#FF21 Bacurau is about colonialism and culture, through the lens of The Most Dangerous Game.

Strange, dark, bloody, funny; it shows people trying to live their very different lives, and the encroaching outsiders. Some stunning imagery.

Very good.

— Gary Fleming (@garyfleming) April 4, 2021

The March Winner

A genuinely difficult month to pick. While Barb & Star is the best comedy I’ve seen in ages, the real contenders are Judas & The Black Messiah and Dead Pigs. I think Judas & The Black Messiah edges it simply because if I had to pick one to watch again soon, it’d be that.
